## SECTION A: COMBO PROSPECTING (10 Questions)

**A1.** What is the COMBO prospecting "triple" and how many should you do per day?

<details>
<summary>Show Answer</summary>

The triple is: (1) Phone call, (2) Voice mail, (3) Email — done in rapid succession. You should do **30 per day**, first thing in the morning from 7:45-8:45 a.m. Each triple should take 2 minutes or less. Follow up each with LinkedIn InMail and a text message.
</details>

---

**A2.** What should you NEVER discuss on your initial prospecting calls? What should you discuss instead?

<details>
<summary>Show Answer</summary>

Never discuss your **products or services.** Instead, deliver a "business-value narrative" — focus on the commercial value you can deliver to the prospect's business. Lead with insight about their business problems and why solving them should be a priority.
</details>

**C3.** Explain the difference between a Capital Lease and an Operating Lease.

<details>
<summary>Show Answer</summary>

**Capital Lease:** Designed for ownership. Customer can purchase equipment at end of term (often at a pre-determined price). Equipment typically goes on the customer's balance sheet.

**Operating Lease:** Designed for use without ownership. Lower monthly payments. At end of term, customer can: (1) return equipment, (2) purchase at fair market value, or (3) renew the lease. Helps businesses manage cash flow and keeps equipment off the balance sheet.
</details>

---

**C4.** What are the three options at the end of an FMV Lease?

<details>
<summary>Show Answer</summary>

(1) Return the equipment with notice, (2) Buy the equipment at fair market value, (3) Continue making rental payments.
</details>

**C7.** What is ABL? What assets support it and what's the range?

<details>
<summary>Show Answer</summary>

**Asset-Based Lending** — a revolving credit facility secured by a company's assets: accounts receivable, inventory, and equipment. Range: **$1MM to $10MM.** Typical advance rates: up to 90% of A/R, 80% on NOLV of inventory, 90% on FLV of equipment.
</details>

**C8.** What is an EBR and how does it differ from ABL?

<details>
<summary>Show Answer</summary>

**Equipment Backed Revolver** — a revolving credit facility secured specifically by equipment and machinery only (not A/R or inventory like ABL). Range: **$500K to $10MM.** Advance rates: up to 100% on Forced Liquidation Value of equipment.
</details>

## SECTION D: COMMON OBJECTIONS (8 Questions)

**D1.** A prospect asks "What's your rate?" — What do you say?

<details>
<summary>Show Answer</summary>

"Well that's exactly why I would like to meet you, since we offer **tailor-made financing solutions**, instead of flat rates. Would you be available to meet either Monday afternoon or Wednesday morning?"

Never give a flat rate — it commoditizes you. Position as a custom solutions provider.
</details>

---

**D2.** A prospect says "I already have other financing companies." — What's your approach?

<details>
<summary>Show Answer</summary>

"That's a good thing! Every company should have several financing options that allows you to offer the most to your customers." Position MHCCA as complementary — servicing areas their current lenders can't. Ask for a meeting to explain the added value.
</details>

---

**D3.** A prospect says "I had a bad experience with Hitachi Capital." — How do you handle it?

<details>
<summary>Show Answer</summary>

Apologize sincerely. Acknowledge the company went through significant growth and changes. Explain that you've worked hard to fix operational processes. Offer to meet and show them the improvements firsthand.
</details>

---

**D4.** What is the "alternative close" technique used in the objection responses?

<details>
<summary>Show Answer</summary>

Instead of asking "Can we meet?" (yes/no question), offer **two specific time options**: "Are you available Monday afternoon or Wednesday morning?" Both answers are a "yes" to meeting — the prospect just picks which time works better.
</details>

---

**D5.** A prospect says "Our customers pay cash." What's the reframe?

<details>
<summary>Show Answer</summary>

Acknowledge that some customers can pay cash, but there are **many benefits to financing even for cash-rich customers** (preserving working capital, tax benefits, flexibility, off-balance-sheet treatment). Ask to meet to discuss these benefits.
</details>

---

**D6.** A prospect says "Just send me documents." How do you handle this?

<details>
<summary>Show Answer</summary>

Agree to send documents, but explain you want to **optimize their time** by going through the information together so all questions are addressed immediately. Mention you'll be in their area and ask for a specific meeting time.
</details>

---

**D7.** What is the three-step pattern every objection response follows?

<details>
<summary>Show Answer</summary>

1. **Acknowledge/empathize** with the objection (never argue)
2. **Reframe** the situation to show value or create a reason to meet
3. **Close for a specific meeting time** using the alternative close technique
</details>

---

**D8.** A prospect says "I don't have time." What's the key reframe?

<details>
<summary>Show Answer</summary>

Respect their time, then reframe the meeting as a **time-saving investment**: "By investing a small amount of your time talking with me, I am confident you will be able to save far more time for your customers with the quality products we offer."
</details>
