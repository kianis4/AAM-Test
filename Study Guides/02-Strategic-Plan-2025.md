## WHAT IS THIS DOCUMENT ABOUT?

This is the company's roadmap for 2025. It answers three big questions:

1. **Where is the parent company (MHC Group) headed long-term?**
2. **What direction is the Canadian business specifically taking?**
3. **What are the financial targets for FY2025?**

Think of it as the "game plan" — what MHCCA (Mitsubishi HC Capital Canada) wants to achieve this year and how it plans to get there. As an associate, you need to understand this because everything you do in sales, credit, and operations should align with these objectives.

## SECTION 1: THE BIG PICTURE — MHC Group 10-Year Vision

### The Vision Statement

> **"Together we INNOVATE, CHALLENGE and EXPLORE the frontiers of the future."**

Three words to remember: **Innovate. Challenge. Explore.** This is the parent company's motto.

### The 3-Phase Plan (MTMPs)

The parent company has a 10-year vision broken into three phases called **MTMPs (Medium-Term Management Plans)**:

| Phase | Name | Timeframe | Goal |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1st MTMP | **HOP** | ~2022-2025 | "Sowing seeds" and "gaining a solid foothold" |
| 2nd MTMP | **STEP** | ~2026-2028 | Building on the foundation |
| 3rd MTMP | **JUMP** | ~2029-2032 | Leaping toward the 10-year vision |

**Plain English:** Think of it like planting a garden. Right now (HOP phase), we're planting seeds and making sure they take root. In the STEP phase, the plants start growing. In the JUMP phase, we harvest.

**We are currently in the HOP phase** — this means 2025 is about establishing foundations, not yet about explosive growth.

### The Three Transformation Pillars

The MHC Group's strategy rests on three big transformations:

#### 1. SX — Sustainability Transformation

- Achieve co-existence and co-prosperity with environmental and social value
- Business strategies based on materiality (what actually matters most)
- Build trust with stakeholders

#### 2. DX — Digital Transformation

- Use data and digital technologies to improve customer value
- Develop DX talent
- Build frameworks for aggressive digital innovation
- Develop new digital businesses

#### 3. Business Portfolio Transformation (CX)

The formula is: **SX + DX + Business Portfolio Transformation = CX (Corporate Transformation)**

Portfolio Management means:

- **Withdraw** from businesses that won't contribute value in the future
- **Optimize** the portfolio for sustainable growth
- **Evolve management** to focus on medium- to long-term returns
- **Pursue capital efficiency** through balance sheet optimization

## SECTION 2: MHCA (AMERICA) — 2025 Objectives

The North American business (which includes Canada) has three big objectives:

### Objective 1: Stabilize the Platform

- **Stem/stop losses** in the historical transportation portfolio
- **Implement new credit models**
- **Improve inventory monitoring** and liquidation strategies
- **Enhance strategic risk oversight** across the enterprise

**Plain English:** The transportation portfolio (trucking, trailers, etc.) has been causing losses. Step one is to stop the bleeding, tighten credit standards, and watch inventory more carefully.

### Objective 2: Align as One North American Business

- Move away from **functional geographic silos** (where Canada and US operated separately)
- **Evolve operations, credit, policies, and processes** to increase consistency
- **Eliminate redundant activities** and streamline operations

**Plain English:** Canada and the US used to run like separate companies. Now they're merging into one coordinated North American platform. Same policies, same processes, no duplication.

### Objective 3: Reengage on Sales Execution and Asset Optimization

- **Drive growth mandate** across MHCA, aligned with the diversification strategy
- Use more **rigorous analyses** to screen attractive markets
- Execute **calculated, industry-focused sales strategies**
- **Closely monitor sales effectiveness** and other key indicators

**Plain English:** Time to get back to growth, but smart growth. Don't just sell anything to anyone — target the right industries with the right products and track how well sales is performing.

## SECTION 3: CANADIAN TRANSFORMATION — Current State vs. Future State

This is a really important section. It shows where MHCCA has been and where it's going.

### Current State (Where We Were)

- Operating as a **legacy Canadian business** (old-school, standalone)
- Portfolio **concentrated in one industry**: Transportation
- **Subject to industry cyclicality** — when trucking goes down, everything goes down
- Product offering was primarily **"1.0 solutions"**: capital leases and loans
- Go-to-market approach: **dealership relationships and small ticket transactions**
- Mainly targeting **near & subprime customers**

### Future State (Where We're Going)

- **One Company:** Strategic alignment across North America (not just Canada alone)
- **Well diversified portfolio** with lower concentration in Transportation
- **Stable revenue sources** to offset cyclical industries
- **Stable, less volatile business** producing consistent income
- **Integrated, diversified North American products offering**
- **Value creation through customer experience** and increased agility
- **Enhanced ROA** (Return on Assets)

**Why this matters for your test:** If Siraaj asks "What's the biggest change in the Canadian business?" the answer is the shift from being a **transportation-heavy, dealership-focused, small-ticket lender** to becoming a **diversified, multi-industry, full-product-suite financial services platform.**

## SECTION 4: THE DUAL APPROACH TO GROWTH

MHCCA is growing in two directions simultaneously:

### Product Diversification (New Products)

These are the new types of financing being added:

- **ABL** (Asset-Based Lending)
- **Senior Secured Cashflow Loans**
- **FMV Lease + Services**
- **Dealer Owned Rental Fleet (DORF)**
- **Servicing platform and Private Label**
- **EaaS** (Equipment-as-a-Service)

### Industry Diversification (New Industries)

Instead of just Transportation, MHCCA is expanding into:

- **Technology**
- **Healthcare**
- **Industrial**
- **Agriculture**

**Visual:** Imagine a Venn diagram. The left circle is "Product Diversification" and the right circle is "Industry Diversification." In the middle (the overlap) is the goal: **Diversified portfolio, stable income producing, less volatile business, value creation through unique products offering.**

## SECTION 5: 2025 STRATEGIC OBJECTIVES & PORTFOLIO MIX

### The Big Target

**Targeting $2.8B portfolio** with **$1,075MM in new business volume** in FY25.

### Portfolio Evolution by Industry (KNOW THESE NUMBERS)

| Segment | FYE 2023 | FY 2025 Target |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Transportation HD (Heavy Duty) | 43% | **19%** (massive reduction) |
| Transportation MD (Medium Duty) | 18% | **20%** |
| Construction | 28% | **38%** (big growth) |
| Diversified (Industrial, Tech Finance, Corporate Aircraft, SDG) | 11% | **23%** (big growth) |

**The story here:** Transportation HD is being cut from 43% to 19%. That's the "pulling back on HD transportation volume" the plan mentions. Meanwhile, Construction and Diversified segments are growing significantly.

### How They're Getting There

#### Stabilize the Portfolio

- Exited less profitable Transportation HD relationships (based on BDE, L2B, NBV and NI)
- Involve sales team in delinquent files over $100k to support collections
- Maintain agility — limit Transportation volume while diversifying

#### Drive Commercial Excellence

- Lift the framework for optimal sales force effectiveness: **CRM, pipeline tracking, high-priority prospect coverage, KPIs measurement, training**
- Use business intelligence and analytics to support new products/industries
- Leverage DX capabilities to enhance customer experience (e.g., the online portal)

#### Execute the MTMP / Transformation Strategy

- Diversify the business
- Offer new value-added products
- Enhance Customer Experience
- Funding/liability management

#### Leverage Key Growth Initiatives

- Establish a solid footprint in **ABL and Private Credit** sectors
- Grow and diversify the **inventory finance platform** through new markets
- Create synergies through the **NA OEM Center of Excellence (COE)**
- Capitalize on new **industry-specific teams** and maximize NA expertise
