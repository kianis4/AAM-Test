## BUSINESS LINE 1: EQUIPMENT FINANCE

### What Is It?

Equipment Finance is the bread and butter of MHCCA. It helps businesses **acquire, refinance, and manage equipment** — everything from trucks to excavators to forklifts. MHCCA leverages its industry expertise to offer flexible financial solutions to OEMs (manufacturers), vendors (dealers), and end-users (the businesses that actually use the equipment).

### The Five Products

#### 1. Capital Lease

**What it is:** A lease designed for ownership. The customer makes payments over the lease term and has the option to **purchase the equipment at the end** — often at a pre-determined price.

**Who it's for:** Companies that **want to eventually own** the equipment.

**Example:** A construction company leases 5 excavators on a 60-month capital lease. At the end, they buy them for $1 each (a "$1 buyout" lease). For accounting purposes, this is treated almost like a purchase — the equipment goes on their balance sheet.

#### 2. Operating Lease

**What it is:** A lease for **using equipment without owning it.** Lower monthly payments than a capital lease. At the end of the lease, the company has three choices:
- **Return** the equipment
- **Purchase** it at fair market value (FMV)
- **Renew** the lease

**Who it's for:** Companies that want **flexibility** — they don't want to be stuck with aging equipment. This is great for tech-heavy or fast-changing industries.

**Why it matters:** Operating leases help businesses manage cash flow by reducing upfront costs and eliminating the burden of ownership. The equipment **stays off the lessee's balance sheet** (which is a big deal for their financial ratios).

**Example:** A logistics company leases 20 trailers on a 48-month operating lease. When the lease ends, trucking demand has shifted, so they return the trailers instead of buying them. They saved themselves from being stuck with depreciating assets.

#### 3. Loan

**What it is:** A straightforward loan — the company gets upfront capital for equipment purchases and repays over time with a predictable schedule.

**Who it's for:** Companies that **want to own the equipment from day one** and prefer a simple repayment structure.

**Example:** A manufacturing company borrows $500K to buy a CNC machine. They make monthly payments over 48 months and own the machine outright from the start.

#### 4. Line of Credit

**What it is:** Flexible financing that lets businesses **draw funds as needed** for equipment acquisition. Think of it like a credit card for equipment purchases — you only pay interest on what you draw.

**Who it's for:** Businesses with **varying equipment needs** or managing multiple projects simultaneously.

**Example:** A healthcare company has a $1M line of credit with MHCCA. In January they draw $200K for new imaging equipment. In March they draw another $300K for patient monitoring systems. They only pay interest on the $500K they've actually used.

#### 5. Refinancing

**What it is:** Allows an end-user to leverage **existing equipment** and the hidden equity within it. They can structure a tailor-made transaction to inject working capital, consolidate debt, reduce monthly payments, or purchase a business.

**Who it's for:** Companies that already own equipment and want to unlock its value.

**Example:** A transportation company owns a fleet of reefer trailers worth $1.1M (with credit rating BB-). MHCC refinances at 100% of OLV (Orderly Liquidation Value) on a 24/48-month term/amortization. The company gets cash to improve its debt service coverage and cash position.

## BUSINESS LINE 2: INVENTORY FINANCE

### What Is It?

Inventory Finance is MHCCA's **flagship service.** It helps OEMs and distributors finance their inventory — the equipment sitting on dealer lots waiting to be sold. Think of it as a bank for dealerships: we pay the manufacturer for the equipment upfront, and the dealer pays us back when they sell it.

This supports the **entire supply chain uniformly across North America.**

### The Two Products

#### 1. Floorplan Financing

**What it is:** Enables dealers to **finance their inventory** — the units sitting on their lot. We pay the manufacturer, the dealer gets the equipment, and when a customer buys a unit, the dealer pays us back.

**Key detail:** Provides **100% advance rates, including taxes,** improving the dealer's purchasing power, cash flow, and sales outcomes.

**Example:** A construction equipment dealer gets 10 new excavators from the OEM. Instead of paying $2M upfront, MHCCA floorplans them at 100% advance rate. When the dealer sells each excavator to an end-customer, they pay MHCCA back. The dealer never had to come up with the $2M.

#### 2. Dealer Owned Rental Fleet (DORF) Financing

**What it is:** Tailored for dealers managing rental fleets. We finance new equipment acquisitions that dealers use in their rental programs.

**Who it's for:** Dealers who rent out equipment (not just sell it) and need financing to grow their rental fleet.

**Example:** A trailer dealer has a rental fleet of 100 units and wants to add 50 more. MHCCA provides DORF financing so the dealer can acquire the new units without massive upfront cost, allowing better fleet management and cash flow optimization.

## BUSINESS LINE 3: COMMERCIAL FINANCE

### What Is It?

Commercial Finance provides **short-term revolving credit** for businesses going through transitions or experiencing growth. Instead of a fixed-term loan, this is like a **credit line backed by the company's own assets** (their receivables, inventory, and equipment).

### The Two Products

#### 1. Asset-Based Lending (ABL)

**What it is:** A revolving credit facility secured by a company's assets — typically **accounts receivable (A/R), inventory, and equipment.**

**How it works:** The company can draw and repay as needed (like a credit card) up to a limit based on the value of their assets.

**Key details:**
- Supported by A/R, inventory, and equipment
- Range: **$1MM - $10MM**
- Used for short-term financing needs

**Typical advance rates for ABL:**
- Up to **90% of A/R**
- **80% on NOLV** (Net Orderly Liquidation Value) of inventory
- **90% on FLV** (Forced Liquidation Value) of equipment

**Example:** A manufacturer hit hard by COVID in 2021-2022 needed an alternative to their bank line. MHCCA provided an ABL line of credit of $2.1M with 85% advance rate on A/R and 65% advance rate on cost of inventories (capped at $800K). Securities included PG of 2 owners, CG of 3 related companies, A/R, Inventories, and First rank on a building worth $1.4M.

#### 2. Equipment Backed Revolver (EBR)

**What it is:** A revolving credit facility secured specifically by a company's **equipment and machinery.** Similar to ABL but narrower — equipment only.

**Key details:**
- Supported by equipment
- Range: **$500K - $10MM**
- Credit limit tied to appraised value of equipment
- Used for short-term financing, flexible access to funds

**Advance rates for EBR:**
- Up to **100% on Forced Liquidation Value** of equipment

**Example:** A construction company in business for 50 years was experiencing liquidity issues due to a contract running longer and costlier than expected. They needed something to complement their existing bank line. MHCCA provided a $3M equipment-backed revolver with up to 100% advance on liquidation value of their construction equipment. The client now uses this line occasionally during peak periods to maintain financial stability.

## BUSINESS LINE 4: TECHNOLOGY FINANCE

### What Is It?

Technology Finance provides flexible financing solutions for acquiring technology — whether for internal use, resale to end users, or channel distribution. It helps clients stay on the cutting edge of technology while **optimizing budgets and preserving cash flow.**

### The Four Products

#### 1. Capital Leases ($1-buyout, Full Lease)

**What it is:** Title stays with MHCCA throughout the term, then transfers to the end-user for a **nominal amount ($1).** The end-user effectively owns it at the end.

**Example:** A company leases 50 laptops on a 36-month capital lease. At the end, they buy all 50 for $1 each. The equipment was always treated as "theirs" on the balance sheet.

#### 2. IPA — Installment Payment Agreements (for software)

**What it is:** Similar to a loan. Title transfers from vendor to customer at point of sale. Taxes can be capitalized or paid upfront.

**Who it's for:** Software purchases specifically — this is how you finance multi-year software licenses.

**Example:** A company wants a 3-year cybersecurity XDR license costing $1M. MHCC provides a 3-year IPA so they can take advantage of the multi-year discount without blowing their IT budget in year one. Payments are spread across the 3-year term.

#### 3. FMV Leases (Fair Market Value Lease)

**What it is:** Provides **lower payments** and maximum flexibility. Title stays with MHCCA. At end-of-term, the customer has **3 options:**
1. **Return** the equipment with notice
2. **Buy** the equipment at fair market value (FMV)
3. **Continue** making rental payments

**Who it's for:** Customers who may not want to own the asset at end-of-term, and/or want to **hedge against technology obsolescence.** This is huge in tech because equipment becomes outdated quickly.

**Example:** A managed service provider leases 3 Lenovo server arrays on a 36-month FMV lease ($1.8M deal). At the end, AI has evolved and they need newer servers, so they return the old ones and lease new ones. They were never stuck with outdated technology.

#### 4. Assignment of Receivables

**What it is:** A Managed Services Provider (MSP) assigns their Account Receivable for a particular end-user contract to MHCCA. We advance a net present value of the payment stream. The end-user may or may not know the receivable was assigned.

**Who it's for:** MSPs who want to get paid upfront for long-term service contracts instead of waiting for monthly payments.

## BUSINESS LINE 5: CAPITAL MARKETS FINANCE

### What Is It?

Capital Markets offers **flexible financing and risk-sharing solutions** for partners in equipment finance. This is essentially the **B2B financing arm** — MHCCA works with other financial institutions, brokers, and lending companies to buy, sell, and syndicate portfolios of leases and loans.

Think of it this way: If Equipment Finance sells directly to businesses, Capital Markets sells to (and buys from) **other financiers.**

### The Three Products

#### 1. Syndication Buy / Sell Desk

**What it is:** Syndication allows financial institutions to share risk through bilateral agreements. MHCCL acts as **both Purchaser and Seller.**

- **As Purchaser:** MHCCL acquires third-party deals, managing risk through cash flow discounting. This lets them enter new industries without direct expertise.
- **As Seller:** MHCCL capitalizes on transactions by selling them immediately, generating revenue without retaining risk. Can also deleverage certain sectors.

#### 2. Portfolio Purchase

**What it is:** MHCCL takes over obligations of various leases and loans from third parties. This allows the partner to **reduce exposure in certain industries** or leave an industry altogether. MHCCL also offers servicing capabilities for purchased portfolios.

#### 3. Funding Facility

**What it is:** Provides financing to companies or institutional investors who need capital for strategic investments, acquisitions, growth, or to manage cash flow and optimize their balance sheet.

### Capital Markets Key Words and Phrases

- Assignment Programs
- Portfolio Acquisitions
- Syndication (Non-Notification) — Buy and Sell Side
- Broker Desk
- Portfolio Servicing
- Warehouse Facilities

### Target Market & Channels

Manufacturing industries, Construction companies, Commercial and Industrial sectors, SMEs, Large corporations

### Industry Sectors

Transportation, Construction, Manufacturing, Technology, Industrial, Material Handling, Healthcare, Forestry, Agriculture

## BUSINESS LINE 7: SUSTAINABLE FINANCE

### What Is It?

Sustainable Finance is about financing equipment and projects that support the **Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) of the United Nations.** The purpose is to help customers reach their sustainability initiatives: reducing carbon intensity, saving energy, reducing water consumption, and encouraging development of new SDG equipment.

### Three Core Strengths

#### 1. Decarbonization of Commercial Mobility

Leases, Loans, As-a-Service for **Class 1-8 ZEVs** (Zero Emission Vehicles) and related infrastructure.

**Examples:** EV school buses and chargers, hydrogen trucks and hydrogen refuellers

#### 2. Clean Energy

Project finance, leases, loans, EaaS for energy projects that are **lower carbon intensity than the local grid.** Preference for renewable energy at **microgrid scale (<75MW).**

**Examples:** Solar installations, biogas plants, microgrids

#### 3. Efficiency Technology

Leases, Loans, As-a-Service for equipment that helps customers **reduce energy consumption, water consumption, and overall carbon footprint.**

**Examples:** Building automation systems, smart building technology, energy-efficient HVAC

### Sustainable Finance Key Words and Phrases

Sustainability, SDG, ESG, Carbon Reduction, Energy Savings, Renewables, Green Energy, Hydrogen, Electrolysers, ZEV, EV, EV Chargers, Infrastructure, Smart Buildings, Building Automation

### Target Market & Channels

OEMs, EPC (Engineering, Procurement, Construction), Developers, Strategic ZEV dealers, Canada Infrastructure Bank/Bank Infrastructure de Canada (CIB/BIC)

### Industry Sectors

Last-mile delivery and drayage, Transportation (Strategic OEM accounts), School Bus (Private Operators), Building Retrofit partners, Power Generation (microgrid, renewables, biogas, carbon reduction), EV Charging ecosystems, Clean technologies

### Case Study — SDG Mobility Consulting

## PRACTICE QUESTIONS & ANSWERS

**Q1: Name all seven business lines in the MHCCA Product Guidebook.**
> **A:** Equipment Finance, Inventory Finance, Commercial Finance, Technology Finance, Capital Markets Finance, Aviation Finance, Sustainable Finance.

**Q2: What is the difference between a Capital Lease and an Operating Lease?**
> **A:** A Capital Lease is designed for ownership — the customer can purchase the equipment at a pre-determined price at the end of the lease term. An Operating Lease is for using equipment without ownership — at the end, the customer can return it, purchase at fair market value, or renew the lease. Operating leases have lower monthly payments and keep equipment off the balance sheet.

**Q3: What are the three options at the end of an FMV (Operating/Fair Market Value) Lease?**
> **A:** (1) Return the equipment with notice, (2) Buy the equipment at fair market value, (3) Continue making rental payments.

**Q4: What is Floorplan Financing?**
> **A:** It enables dealers to finance their inventory. MHCCA pays the manufacturer for equipment delivered to the dealer. When the dealer sells a unit to an end-customer, they repay MHCCA. It provides 100% advance rates including taxes.

**Q5: What is DORF?**
> **A:** Dealer Owned Rental Fleet financing — tailored for dealers managing rental fleets, financing new equipment acquisitions for their rental programs.

**Q6: What is the difference between ABL and EBR?**
> **A:** ABL (Asset-Based Lending) is a revolving credit facility secured by a company's broader assets — A/R, inventory, AND equipment (range: $1MM-$10MM). EBR (Equipment Backed Revolver) is a revolving credit facility secured only by equipment and machinery (range: $500K-$10MM). ABL is broader; EBR is equipment-specific.

**Q7: What are the typical advance rates for ABL?**
> **A:** Up to 90% of A/R, 80% on NOLV of inventory, 90% on FLV of equipment.

**Q8: What is an IPA and when is it used?**
> **A:** Installment Payment Agreement — used specifically for software purchases. Title transfers from vendor to customer at point of sale. Taxes can be capitalized or paid upfront. It allows customers to spread software license costs over multiple years.

**Q9: What is the key selling point of Inventory Finance?**
> **A:** 24-hour funding, 100% advance rates including taxes, North American standardized approach, and no need for the OEM to have their own collection team.

**Q10: What are the four pillars of Aviation Finance?**
> **A:** (1) Traditional Flight Equipment (Turbine, Turboprop, Piston, Helicopter), (2) Ground Support Equipment, (3) Flight and Cabin Simulators, (4) Emerging Technologies (SAF, Electrification, Hydrogen, Drones).

**Q11: What are the three core strengths of Sustainable Finance?**
> **A:** (1) Decarbonization of Commercial Mobility (ZEVs), (2) Clean Energy (renewables at microgrid scale <75MW), (3) Efficiency Technology (equipment reducing energy/water consumption).

**Q12: In the Syndication Buy/Sell desk, what's the difference between MHCCL acting as Purchaser vs. Seller?**
> **A:** As Purchaser, MHCCL acquires third-party deals through cash flow discounting to enter new industries without direct expertise. As Seller, MHCCL capitalizes on transactions by selling them immediately, generating revenue without retaining risk and deleveraging certain sectors.

**Q13: Name the five Equipment Finance products.**
> **A:** Capital Lease, Operating Lease, Loan, Line of Credit, Refinancing.
