# PART 3: THE 4 PRODUCTS [!!]

> **TL;DR:** Know the 7 business lines, but focus on the 4 you'll use daily: Capital Lease, Operating Lease, Loan, and FMV Lease. Know how they differ.

---

## The Product Comparison You MUST Know

| Feature | Capital Lease ($1 Buyout) | Operating Lease (FMV) | Loan |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Who owns the equipment?** | Client at end ($1 buyout) | MHCCA (client returns or buys at FMV) | Client from day 1 |
| **On client's balance sheet?** | YES | **NO** (if 4 criteria met) | YES |
| **Depreciation by client?** | YES | NO | YES |
| **What's tax deductible?** | Full lease payment | Full lease payment | **Interest ONLY** (not principal) |
| **Interest shown separately?** | YES | NO (whole payment = operating expense) | YES |

> **Exam trap:** On a loan, only the INTEREST is tax deductible. On a lease (capital or operating), the FULL payment is deductible. This is a common trick question.

---

## [!!!] Off-Balance Sheet — The 4 Criteria

For an operating lease to stay OFF the client's balance sheet, **ALL FOUR** must be met:

| # | Rule | Threshold | Memory |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Lease term vs useful life | **< 75%** | "Three-quarters" |
| 2 | PV of payments vs FMV | **< 90%** | "Ninety percent" |
| 3 | Bargain purchase option | **NONE** | "No $1 buyout" |
| 4 | Auto transfer of ownership | **NONE** | "No free handover" |

**Memory shortcut: "75 — 90 — No — No"**

**If even ONE criterion fails, the lease goes ON the balance sheet.**

### The 20% Residual Rule (MHCCA Internal Policy)
- The residual value must be **higher than 20%** of the equipment value
- This is MORE conservative than the 90% accounting rule
- It ensures MHCCA has real skin in the game on the asset

## LEAD — The 4 Steps

| Step | What It Means | In Practice |
|---|---|---|
| **L — Legitimize** | Earn the right to be there | Introduce yourself, explain MHCCA, set the agenda ("I'd like to take 40 min to understand your business and show how we can help") |
| **E — Explore** | Ask questions, understand their world | Open-ended questions about their business, fleet, financing, pain points. Listen more than you talk |
| **A — Accentuate** | Show why MHCCA is different | Present solutions tailored to what you learned in Explore. Use product knowledge, industry expertise |
| **D — Decide** | Get a commitment | Close for next steps — a deal submission, a follow-up meeting, a financial package from the client |

### If the Client Doesn't Express Needs — Use APEP

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| **A — Affirm** | Start with something positive about their current situation |
| **P — Probe** | Ask deeper questions to uncover hidden needs |
| **E — Empathize** | Show you understand their challenges |
| **P — Propose** | Offer a solution based on what you uncovered |

## [!!] The 9 Objections — Know the Pattern

| # | Objection | Key Phrase in Your Response |

| 9 | "Who are you again?" | Quick, confident intro — name, company, what you do, why you're calling |

> **TL;DR:** The OPS exam is the hardest. It tests whether you know the exact policy rules for documentation. This section is your lifeline.

## The Complete Checklist — Print This in Your Brain

### VOID CHEQUE
- [ ] Complete **legal name** of the lessee
- [ ] Must be an actual **void cheque** — NOT a handwritten PAD form

### CONTRACT
- [ ] Legal name **matches corporate profile exactly**
- [ ] PG name **matches government ID exactly**
- [ ] Signature **matches ID**
- [ ] Equipment description **matches BOS**
- [ ] Vendor name **correct on authorization AND acceptance**
- [ ] **Initials on ALL relevant pages**

### BILL OF SALE (BOS)
- [ ] **Lease** = Billed to **Mitsubishi HC Capital Canada Leasing, Inc**
- [ ] **Loan** = Billed to **the Client**
- [ ] Ship To = **ALWAYS the client** (with their address)
- [ ] Full equipment description (make, model, serial #, year)
- [ ] Cost & taxes correct
- [ ] Deposit noted **AFTER taxes** (not before)
- [ ] Current dated
- [ ] Tax numbers listed (GST/HST/QST)

### SAF (Schedule of Additional Fees)
- [ ] Equipment description is **NOT required** on SAF (only on BOS)

### INSURANCE

**For a LEASE:**
- [ ] Entity: **Mitsubishi HC Capital Canada Leasing, Inc** (note: "Leasing")
- [ ] Designation: **Additional Insured + Loss Payee** OR **5A Endorsement + listed as Lessor**
- [ ] Liability: **$2M minimum**
- [ ] Deductible: **below $25K**

**For a LOAN:**
- [ ] Entity: **Mitsubishi HC Capital Canada, Inc** (note: NO "Leasing")
- [ ] Designation: **Loss Payee** only
- [ ] Liability: **$2M minimum**
- [ ] Deductible: **below $25K**

### ID
- [ ] Clear and legible
- [ ] Name matches contract — **no discrepancies**
- [ ] **Not expired**

## [!!!] The 8 OPS Exam Traps

These are the trick questions. Read each one twice.

| # | The Trap | The Right Answer |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Entity names** — mixing up Leasing vs non-Leasing | Lease = "**Leasing**, Inc" / Loan = "Canada, Inc" |
| 2 | **BOS billing** — assuming it's always the same | Lease = billed to MHCCL / Loan = billed to Client |
| 3 | **PAD form vs void cheque** | Void cheque ONLY. Handwritten PAD = NOT acceptable |
| 4 | **Hazmat/Bus = lease?** | NO. Always a **LOAN.** It's the USE, not the asset type |
| 5 | **Insurance for leases** — saying "loss payee only" | Leases need **Additional Insured + Loss Payee** (or 5A). Loss payee alone = loans only |
| 6 | **Policy vs practice** | Exam tests what the **policy says**, not what people actually do |
| 7 | **Ship-to address on lease BOS** | ALWAYS the **client's address**, even though MHCCA is the buyer. Determines tax jurisdiction |
| 8 | **Deposit placement** | Must be **AFTER taxes**, not before |

## What KYC Means

| Verify | How |

## Fraud Red Flags

| Red Flag | Why It's Suspicious |
|---|---|
| ID doesn't match documents | Name/address/signature discrepancy |
| New business + large request | No track record, big ask |
| Rush to close | Trying to skip due diligence |
| Inconsistent information | Details don't line up |
| Equipment doesn't match business | Flower shop buying excavators |
| Vendor on blocked list | Previously flagged |
| Won't provide documents | What are they hiding? |

## Third-Party Due Diligence

Before accepting deals from a vendor or broker:
- Verify they're a **legitimate, operating business**
- Check the **blocked vendor/broker list** — if blocked, deal is rejected
- Confirm no history of **fraud or compliance violations**
- **Always check blocked lists before submitting any deal**

## FCCR — The Most Important Ratio

**FCCR = Can they pay their bills?**
- Over 1.0x = yes, they can cover fixed charges
- Under 1.0x = no — need mitigants (down payment, PG)
- **Watch for inflated FCCR** when financials don't report CPLTD (common in NTR statements)

## Why FMV Leases > Capital Leases (For MHCCA)

1. **Higher implicit yields** — rate is embedded, not visible, less price competition
2. **Off-balance sheet for clients** — major selling point for large companies
3. **Evergreen potential** — client keeps paying after term on a fully depreciated asset = pure profit
4. **Remarketing upside** — MHCCA sells the returned equipment for more than the conservative residual

## Evergreen Leases — Pure Profit

After an FMV lease term ends, clients often **keep paying month-to-month.** The equipment is fully depreciated on MHCCA's books, so every payment is **near-100% margin.** Common in tech where clients delay equipment refreshes.

## Service-Based Income — The Growth Engine

Revenue MHCCA earns **without adding assets to its balance sheet:**
- Portfolio servicing (managing other lenders' portfolios for fees)
- Consulting and advisory
- Private label programs
- Technology/credit scoring

Why it matters: Improves ROA without growing the denominator (assets).

## Short Answer — 2-Point Questions (Write Exactly This for Full Marks)

**Q1:** What are the four criteria for an operating lease to qualify for off-balance sheet treatment?

**Full-marks answer:**

> For an operating lease to qualify for off-balance sheet treatment, ALL FOUR of the following criteria must be met:
>
> 1. The lease term must be less than 75% of the asset's useful economic life
> 2. The present value of all lease payments cannot exceed 90% of the asset's fair market value
> 3. There can be no bargain purchase option (no $1 buyout — the purchase option must be at fair market value)
> 4. There can be no automatic transfer of ownership at the end of the lease term
>
> If even one criterion fails, the lease is classified as a capital lease and goes on the client's balance sheet. Additionally, MHCCA's internal policy requires the residual value to be higher than 20% of the equipment value.

---

**Q2:** What are the hard rules for Transport Class 8 OTR deals? List all requirements that must be met.

**Full-marks answer:**

> Class 8 OTR has the following hard rules that must be met for any deal:
>
> 1. No auto-decision authority — every Class 8 deal requires manual analyst review regardless of amount
> 2. First-time owner/operators are NOT eligible — this is a hard rule with no exceptions
> 3. All borrowers must be verified homeowners
> 4. Minimum 5 years in business
> 5. Maximum term is 60 months
> 6. Only Kenworth and Peterbilt are Tier 1 (Volvo, Freightliner, and International are Tier 3)
> 7. For used equipment, term deductions apply: subtract 12 months per model year or per 200,000 km, whichever is more restrictive
> 8. CR4 used equipment in Ontario and Western provinces is NOT eligible (only eligible in Quebec and Maritimes with 10-15% down)

---

**Q3:** What is the difference in insurance requirements between a lease and a loan? Be specific about entity names and designations.

**Full-marks answer:**

> For a LEASE: The insurance must name Mitsubishi HC Capital Canada Leasing, Inc as Additional Insured and Loss Payee, OR alternatively include a 5A Endorsement (SEF 5A) with MHCCA listed as Lessor. This is because MHCCA Leasing owns the equipment in a lease.
>
> For a LOAN: The insurance must name Mitsubishi HC Capital Canada, Inc (without "Leasing") as Loss Payee only. No additional insured designation or 5A endorsement is needed. MHCCA has a security interest (lien) but does not own the equipment.
>
> Both lease and loan require minimum $2 million liability coverage and a deductible below $25,000.
>
> The key distinction is the legal entity: "Leasing, Inc" for leases (MHCCA owns the asset) vs. "Canada, Inc" for loans (client owns the asset, MHCCA holds a lien).

---

**Q4:** Explain the difference between a capital lease and an operating lease from the client's perspective. What is tax deductible under each?

**Full-marks answer:**

> In a capital lease ($1 buyout), the client is effectively purchasing the equipment. The equipment appears as an asset on the client's balance sheet, and the lease obligation appears as a liability. The client records depreciation. The full lease payment (interest + principal) is tax deductible.
>
> In an operating lease (FMV), the client is renting the equipment. The equipment stays OFF the client's balance sheet (if the 4 off-balance sheet criteria are met). No depreciation is recorded by the client. The full lease payment is treated as an operating expense and is fully tax deductible.
>
> For comparison, on a loan, only the INTEREST portion is tax deductible — the principal repayment is NOT deductible.
>
> The key advantage of an operating lease for the client is off-balance sheet treatment (improves debt ratios) and flexibility (return, buy at FMV, or continue payments at end of term). The key advantage of a capital lease is guaranteed ownership at end of term for a nominal $1 buyout.

---

**Q5:** A deal file comes in. The BOS shows the equipment is billed to the client, but the deal is structured as a lease. The insurance names "Mitsubishi HC Capital Canada, Inc" as loss payee. Identify all errors.

**Full-marks answer:**

> There are two errors in this deal file:
>
> 1. BOS billing error: On a lease, the BOS must be billed to Mitsubishi HC Capital Canada Leasing, Inc — NOT the client. The client is billed on a loan because they are the purchaser. On a lease, MHCCA Leasing is purchasing the equipment, so the BOS is billed to MHCCA Leasing.
>
> 2. Insurance entity error: The insurance names "Mitsubishi HC Capital Canada, Inc" but this is a lease. For leases, the entity must be "Mitsubishi HC Capital Canada LEASING, Inc" — with "Leasing" in the name. Additionally, for a lease, MHCCA should be listed as Additional Insured AND Loss Payee (or have a 5A Endorsement with MHCCA as Lessor). Simply listing as "Loss Payee" alone is the loan designation, not the lease designation.
>
> Both errors must be corrected before the deal can proceed. The BOS must be reissued with MHCCL as the billed party, and the insurance certificate must be updated with the correct entity name and designation.
