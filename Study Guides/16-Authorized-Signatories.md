# STUDY GUIDE: Authorized Signatories by Type of Company

### *MHCCA Internal Guideline*

---

## WHAT IS THIS DOCUMENT ABOUT?

This guide answers one of the most critical questions in contract documentation: **"Who is legally allowed to sign this contract?"** The answer depends entirely on what type of company the client is. Get it wrong, and the entire contract could be unenforceable — MHCCA would have financed equipment with no legal recourse if things go south.

Think of it this way: if you bring a contract to a trucking company and the office manager signs it, but the office manager has no signing authority — that contract is worthless. You need the right person to sign based on the company's legal structure.

**Why should you care?** This affects **every single deal you touch.** The responsibility chain starts with Credit (when accepting the application), then passes to whoever documents the contract (Sales, Sales Operation, or Legal). When a partner documents externally (Broker or Syndication), you must validate signing authority at both **V1 and V2 stages.**

---

## SECTION 1: WHO IS RESPONSIBLE FOR GETTING THIS RIGHT?

The responsibility is shared across multiple departments, in this order:

| Priority | Department | Responsibility |
|---|---|---|
| **1st** | **Credit** | Establishing the correct signatory when accepting the application and integrating data in Vision |
| **2nd** | **Sales / Sales Operation / Legal** | The person who documents the contract must verify signing authority |
| **3rd** | **Validation (CVC)** | When contracts are documented externally by a Broker or Syndication, validate at **V1 and V2** |

**Impacted Departments:** Contract Validation Center, Legal, Customer Service, Transfers, Sales, Sales Support, Credit

> **Why This Matters:** If a contract comes back with the wrong signatory, the first question will be "Who should have caught this?" The answer flows from Credit → Documentation → Validation. Know your role in the chain.

---

## SECTION 2: JOINT STOCK COMPANY ("INC.")

This is the **most common** company type you'll encounter.

| Aspect | Details |
|---|---|
| **Concept** | Legally constituted company with distinct patrimony (separate from its owners) |
| **Can do** | Enter into contracts, hold assets, incur debts |
| **Ownership** | Owned by one or more **shareholders**, administered by one or more **directors** |
| **Incorporation** | Provincial (Quebec Inc.) or Federal (Canada Inc.) |
| **Liability** | Unless there is a personal guarantee, shareholders are **NOT liable** for company debts |

### Who Can Sign?

**One or other of the directors** appointed to the REQ (Quebec) or Securfact (Rest of Canada).

### Exception 1: Unanimous Shareholder Agreement

This is the big gotcha. A **unanimous shareholder agreement** can **withdraw signing authority from directors** and assign it to shareholders instead.

| Registry | How to Check |
|---|---|
| **REQ (Quebec)** | The REQ **indicates** the existence of this agreement |
| **Securfact (ROC)** | Does **NOT** indicate this — the customer must notify you |

The REQ will show one of three messages:
1. "Il existe une convention unanime des actionnaires" (There IS one)
2. "Il n'existe pas de convention unanime des actionnaires" (There is NOT one)
3. "Il n'existe plus de convention unanime des actionnaires" (There is NO LONGER one)

**Procedure:** See page 52 of the Credit Risk Policy.

> **Why This Matters:** If a unanimous shareholder agreement exists and you let a director sign, that signature may be invalid because the directors' authority was transferred to shareholders. ALWAYS check the REQ for this. In ROC provinces, you rely on the customer to disclose this — which is riskier.

### Exception 2: Power of Attorney or Signature Resolution

A **third party** can be authorized to sign via a Power of Attorney or signature resolution. This is an **external document** that must be approved by:
- Credit, OR
- Legal, OR
- A CVC (Contract Validation Center) manager

| **Forms** | CON14.21 or CON14.22 |

## SECTION 4: SOLE PROPRIETORSHIP

| Aspect | Details |
|---|---|
| **Concept** | Self-employed — simplest and most inexpensive business form |
| **Distinction** | **NOT distinct** from its owner — same patrimony (inheritance) |
| **Ownership** | Only **one** owner |
| **Registration** | No legal obligation to register at REQ/Securfact, but **MHCCA requires it** to demonstrate financing a company, not a physical person |

### Who Can Sign?

**The owner** — and only the owner.

| Registry | Where to Find the Owner |
|---|---|
| **REQ** | Refer to the "Nom" section → "Nom de la personne physique" |
| **Securfact** | Rely on the "Proprietor information" section |

> **Why This Matters:** A sole proprietorship is the only company type where there's literally one possible signatory. But you still need the REQ/Securfact registration to prove it's a business, not a personal loan.

---

## SECTION 5: GENERAL PARTNERSHIP (G.P.) — QUEBEC ONLY

| Aspect | Details |
|---|---|
| **Concept** | A group of people ("partners") carrying out a common activity (e.g., legal or accounting services) |
| **Liability** | Each partner is **personally liable** for the debts and acts of the company |
| **Who Can Sign** | **ALL associates must sign** and provide a valid ID |
| **REQ** | Refer to the "Associates" section |

> **Why This Matters:** This is Quebec-specific. The key word is ALL — every single partner must sign. Missing one partner's signature could invalidate the contract.

---

## SECTION 6: LIMITED PARTNERSHIP ("L.P.")

This one has **two categories of partners** — and only one category can sign.

| Partner Type | French Term | Role | Liability | Can Sign? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Sponsor** (Limited Partner) | Commanditaire | Contributes money, receives dividends, does **NOT** manage | Limited to their financial contribution | **NO** |
| **Sponsored** (General Partner) | Commandité | Contributes expertise, **manages** the business | **Personally responsible** for all debts | **YES** |

### Who Can Sign?

**One of the sponsored (commandités)** — the general partners who manage the business.

**N.B. A limited partner (sponsor/commanditaire) has NO signing authority.**

> **Why This Matters:** This is a classic test question. Limited partners put in money but CAN'T sign. General partners (sponsored/commandités) run the business and CAN sign. If a limited partner signs your contract, it's invalid.

---

## SECTION 7: SOCIÉTÉ EN PARTICIPATION (S.E.P.) / JOINT VENTURE PARTNERSHIP

| Aspect | Details |
|---|---|
| **Concept** | Rather rare — an informal business relationship between partners |
| **Registration** | No legal obligation to register, but **MHCCA requires it** to finance |
| **Assets** | No separate assets from its partners |
| **Who Can Sign** | **ALL associates must sign** and provide valid ID |

---

## SECTION 8: COOPERATIVE

| Aspect | Details |
|---|---|
| **Concept** | Legal person bringing together people or companies with common economic, social, or cultural needs |
| **Structure** | Two structures: (1) Democratic association (general meeting + board), (2) Corporate structure (GM, admin, employees) |
| **Governance** | Governed by the board of directors, managed by a General Manager appointed by the board |
| **Ownership** | Belongs to ALL members equally — co-owners are the users ("members") |

### Who Can Sign?

**It depends on the cooperative's internal documents.** You must request:

1. **Extract from a resolution** naming the authorized signatories
2. **The Cooperative's Certificate of Conformity**
3. **The internal management regulations**

Then have the **Legal team validate** who has signing authority.

> **Why This Matters:** Cooperatives are the most complex company type for signing authority because there's no universal rule. You MUST get the internal documents and have Legal review them. Never assume who can sign for a cooperative.

---

## SECTION 9: MUNICIPALITY

| Aspect | Details |
|---|---|
| **Concept** | Has a municipal council composed of a mayor and councillors (number varies by city size) |
| **Who Can Sign** | **Administrator at the REQ** OR signatory designated via an **Extract from the resolutions of a council meeting** |

---

## SECTION 10: NON-PROFIT ORGANIZATION (NPO)

| Aspect | Details |
|---|---|
| **Concept** | Associations, clubs, societies operated solely for community welfare, recreation, entertainment, or other non-profit activity |
| **Registration** | **No obligation** to register under REQ/Securfact |
| **Examples** | Social/recreational groups (bridge, curling, golf clubs), amateur sports organizations, festival organizers |
| **Who Can Sign** | **Administrator at the REQ** OR signatory designated via **Excerpt from the resolutions** (MHCCA's form or the client's) |

---

## SECTION 11: "PARTNERSHIP" (REST OF CANADA ONLY)

| Aspect | Details |
|---|---|
| **Concept** | At least 2 people share ownership, management, losses, and income |
| **Reference** | Pages 84+ of the Customer Documentation and Security Policy |

### Three Types of Partnerships (ROC)

| Type | Liability | Who Can Sign |
|---|---|---|
| **General Partnership** | Partners have **100% joint liability** | **ALL partners** must sign |
| **Limited Partnership** | Limited partners' liability = their contribution only | **Only General Partners** can sign |
| **Limited Liability Partnership** | Varies | Refer to Documentation and Security Policy |

---

## SECTION 12: BOARD RESOLUTION FORMS

| Form | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **CON 14.21** | Excerpt from Board resolutions — **MHCCA's form** |
| **CON 14.22** | Resolution provided by **the customer** |

Either form is acceptable. The customer can provide their own resolution format (CON 14.22) or use MHCCA's standard form (CON 14.21).

---

## SECTION 13: QUICK REFERENCE — WHO CAN SIGN?

| Company Type | Who Signs | Key Rule |
|---|---|---|
| **Inc. (Joint Stock)** | Director(s) at REQ/Securfact | Check for unanimous shareholder agreement! |
| **Non-profit Company** | Per Board Resolution (President/Secretary) | Need CON14.21 or CON14.22 |
| **Sole Proprietorship** | The owner only | Must have REQ/Securfact registration |
| **General Partnership (QC)** | ALL associates + valid ID | Everyone signs |
| **Limited Partnership (L.P.)** | One of the sponsored (commandités) | Limited partners (commanditaires) CANNOT sign |
| **S.E.P. / Joint Venture** | ALL associates + valid ID | Everyone signs |
| **Cooperative** | Depends on internal docs | Legal must validate — get 3 documents |
| **Municipality** | Admin at REQ or via council resolution | Need extract from council meeting |
| **NPO** | Admin at REQ or via resolution excerpt | No registration obligation |
| **Partnership (ROC)** | General = ALL; Limited = General Partners only | Same logic as QC L.P. |

---

## SECTION 14: REQ DEFINITIONS — WHAT YOU'LL SEE IN THE REGISTRY

| Term (French) | English | What It Means |
|---|---|---|
| **Actionnaires** | Shareholders | Hold shares; top 3 vote-holders are shown |
| **Convention unanime des actionnaires** | Unanimous shareholder agreement | Can transfer board powers to shareholders |
| **Liste des administrateurs** | List of directors | Current and former directors displayed |
| **Dirigeants non membres du conseil** | Officers not on the board | Involved in administration but not board members |

## PRACTICE QUESTIONS & ANSWERS

Test yourself. Cover the answers and try to answer from memory.

---

### Question 1
**Who has the FIRST responsibility for establishing the correct signatory on a contract?**

**Answer:** **Credit**, when accepting the application and integrating data in Vision. Then responsibility passes to the person documenting the contract (Sales, Sales Operation, or Legal).

---

### Question 2
**A company is incorporated as "ABC Trucking Inc." Who can sign the lease contract?**

**Answer:** One or other of the **directors** appointed to the REQ (Quebec) or Securfact (ROC). However, you must first check whether a **unanimous shareholder agreement** exists, which could transfer signing authority from directors to shareholders.

---

### Question 3
**How do you check for a unanimous shareholder agreement in Quebec vs. Rest of Canada?**

**Answer:**
- **Quebec (REQ):** The REQ **indicates** whether a unanimous shareholder agreement exists
- **Rest of Canada (Securfact):** Securfact does **NOT** indicate this — you must rely on the **customer to notify you**

---

### Question 4
**A Limited Partnership (L.P.) has 3 limited partners (sponsors) and 1 general partner (sponsored). Who can sign?**

**Answer:** Only the **1 general partner (sponsored/commandité)** can sign. The 3 limited partners (sponsors/commanditaires) have **NO signing authority**, regardless of how much money they contributed.

---

### Question 5
**What three documents must you request from a cooperative before determining who can sign?**

**Answer:**
1. Extract from a resolution naming the authorized signatories
2. The Cooperative's Certificate of Conformity
3. The internal management regulations of the Cooperative
Then **Legal** must validate who has signing authority.

---

### Question 6
**A General Partnership in Quebec has 4 partners. How many must sign the contract?**

**Answer:** **All 4** — every associate must sign and provide a valid ID. Missing even one partner's signature could invalidate the contract.

---

### Question 7
**What is the difference between CON 14.21 and CON 14.22?**

**Answer:**
- **CON 14.21:** Excerpt from Board resolutions using **MHCCA's form**
- **CON 14.22:** Resolution provided by **the customer** in their own format

---

### Question 8
**A sole proprietorship wants to lease equipment. The owner says they don't need to be registered at the REQ because sole proprietorships have no legal obligation to register. Are they correct? Can MHCCA proceed?**

**Answer:** The owner is **technically correct** — there is no legal obligation to register. However, **MHCCA requires registration** to demonstrate that it is financing a company and not a physical person. The deal cannot proceed without REQ/Securfact registration.

---

### Question 9
**For an Inc., a third party (not a director or shareholder) wants to sign the contract. Under what conditions is this acceptable?**

**Answer:** A third party can sign if they have a **Power of Attorney or signature resolution** authorizing them. This external document must be approved by **Credit, Legal, or a CVC manager** before it's accepted.

---

### Question 10
**Who can sign for a municipality?**

**Answer:** Either the **Administrator at the REQ** or a **signatory designated via an Extract from the resolutions of a council meeting** of the municipality.

---

### Question 11
**True or False: For an NPO, the organization must be registered under the REQ before MHCCA can finance them.**

**Answer:** **False.** NPOs have **no obligation** to register under the REQ/Securfact. The signatory is either the Administrator at the REQ (if registered) or someone designated via an Excerpt from the resolutions.

---

### Question 12
**A contract was documented externally by a Broker. At what stages must signing authority be validated?**

**Answer:** Signing authority must be validated at **both V1 and V2 stages** when the contract is documented externally by a partner (Broker or Syndication).

---

### Question 13
**What are the 3 messages the REQ can display regarding a unanimous shareholder agreement?**

**Answer:**
1. "Il existe une convention unanime des actionnaires" — There **IS** one
2. "Il n'existe pas de convention unanime des actionnaires" — There is **NOT** one
3. "Il n'existe plus de convention unanime des actionnaires" — There is **NO LONGER** one

---

### Question 14
**In a Limited Partnership, what is the difference between a "commanditaire" and a "commandité"?**

**Answer:**
- **Commanditaire (Sponsor/Limited Partner):** Contributes money, receives dividends, does NOT manage, liability limited to their contribution, **CANNOT sign**
- **Commandité (Sponsored/General Partner):** Contributes expertise, manages the business, personally responsible for debts, **CAN sign**
