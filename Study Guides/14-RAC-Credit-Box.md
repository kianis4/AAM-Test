# STUDY GUIDE: The RAC — MHCCNA's Credit Box (Risk Acceptance Criteria)

---

## WHAT IS THIS DOCUMENT ABOUT?

The RAC (Risk Acceptance Criteria) is the **bible of MHCCNA**. It defines exactly what deals you can and cannot do — for every industry segment, every credit rating, every asset type. When a deal comes across your desk, the RAC tells you: Can we finance this? How much can we approve automatically? What documents do we need? How long can the term be? How old can the equipment be?

Think of the RAC as a rulebook with 20+ chapters — one for each business segment (Construction, Forestry, Transportation, Agriculture, etc.). Each chapter lays out the **guardrails** for that segment: who the target customer is, what credit rating they need, what assets are eligible, how deals should be structured, and what exceptions require management approval.

**Effective Date:** September 16, 2025 (Version 3)

## 1. UNIVERSAL CONCEPTS YOU NEED TO KNOW FIRST

Before diving into any specific RAC, you need to understand the building blocks that every single RAC uses.

### Credit Ratings (CR1 through CR5)

Every customer gets a credit rating determined by the **Credit Scorecard** or **BTA (Business Threat Assessment)**. This rating drives EVERYTHING — what terms they get, how much down payment, whether the deal can be auto-approved, and sometimes whether we can even do the deal at all.

| Rating | What It Means | Think of It As |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **CR1** | Excellent credit — strong financials, long history, clean record | The "dream customer" |
| **CR2** | Very good credit — solid business, reliable payment history | Still a great deal |
| **CR3** | Average credit — acceptable risk, may need more documentation | The bread-and-butter customer |
| **CR4** | Below average — higher risk, needs more down payment & security | Proceed with caution |
| **CR5** | Weak credit — many segments won't touch this at all | Very restricted or declined |

**Why This Matters:** The credit rating is the single most important factor in every deal. It determines your auto-decision limit, max amortization, down payment requirement, and whether you even need manager approval.

### Comparable Credit (CC)

**Comparable Credit** means the customer has an existing, active credit history that is **similar in size** to what they're requesting. It's proof they can handle debt of this magnitude.

| Deal Size | CC Requirement |
| --- | --- |
| Less than $50,000 | 50% comparable credit |
| Less than $100,000 | 75% comparable credit |
| $100,000 and above | 100% comparable credit |

**Example:** A customer wants to finance $200,000 of equipment. To satisfy the 100% CC requirement, they need to show existing credit lines of at least $200,000 on their commercial bureau (Equifax/PayNet).

### Equipment Tiers (Tier 1, 2, 3)

Equipment manufacturers are grouped into tiers based on brand reputation, resale value, and market presence in Canada. The tier affects how long you can finance the asset and how much residual value it holds.

| Tier | What It Means | Impact on Deal |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Tier 1** | Premium, well-known OEMs with strong resale markets | Longest terms, best residuals, most flexibility |
| **Tier 2** | Established mid-market brands with decent resale | Moderate terms, some restrictions |
| **Tier 3** | Overseas or niche brands with limited Canadian presence | Shortest terms, worst resale, most restrictions |

### Auto-Decision Limits

An **auto-decision** is a deal that can be approved automatically by the system (or at the analyst level) without requiring manager review. Each RAC specifies auto-decision limits by credit rating.

**Example (Construction):**
- CR1/CR2: Up to $375K auto-decision
- CR3: Up to $200K auto-decision
- CR4: $0 auto-decision (every deal needs manual review)

### Personal Guarantee (PG) & Personal Net Worth (PNW)

A **Personal Guarantee** means the business owner personally backs the deal — if the company defaults, the individual is on the hook. A **Signed PNW** (Personal Net Worth Statement) documents what the guarantor owns and owes.

- PG is typically **required** for CR3 and CR4
- The guarantor's personal credit score (CRP/FICO) must usually be **above 650**
- CR1/CR2 often at **credit's discretion**

### Financial Statement Requirements by Deal Size

This pattern repeats across almost every RAC:

| Exposure | Typical Requirements |
| --- | --- |
| Under $50K | Application only, minimal docs |
| $50K – $250K | App only, comparable credit required, PG if needed |
| $250K – $500K | Financial statements start becoming required |
| $500K – $1MM | Full FS (3 years accountant-prepared + interim), bank statements |
| Over $1MM | **Structured Credit Application** — BTA rating, 4Block spreads, AR/AP, org chart, borrowing base, projections |

### IOP (Interim Operating Period) / Skip Payments

An **IOP** is a period where the customer doesn't make payments — typically used for seasonal businesses (construction companies that can't work in winter, farmers who only earn at harvest). Key rules:
- IOPs are **not permitted in the first 6 months** unless program-approved or offset by an equivalent upfront payment
- The IOP **must not extend the amortization** of the deal
- Different from "skip payments" — IOPs are structured at inception, skips are ad hoc

### DORF (Dealer Owned Rental Fleet)

A **DORF** is when a dealer finances equipment through us specifically to rent it out to end-users (not to use themselves). This is considered **higher risk** because:
- The end-user isn't our customer
- Rental equipment gets abused more
- Recovery is harder if things go wrong

Most RACs restrict DORF/rental house transactions to **$250K max** and **MHCC reps only — no brokers**.

## 2. RESTRICTED INDUSTRIES & PROHIBITED DEALS

Before you even look at a deal, check if it falls into a prohibited category. MHCCA follows MHC Group's global compliance guidelines.

### Absolutely Prohibited — Will Not Finance Under Any Circumstances:

1. **Weapons manufacturing or sales** (including cluster bombs, nuclear, biological, chemical weapons, killer robots/drones)
2. **Organized crime** — any business with ties to organized crime groups or front companies
3. **Oil & Gas transactions above $1MM USD** (per Japan policy)
4. **Cryptocurrency** — any transactions related to crypto
5. **Cannabis industry** — no contracts related to cannabis
6. **Gaming activities** (gambling)
7. **Usury / money laundering / piracy / trademark violations**
8. **Obscene materials production**
9. **Consumer or personal use** — all equipment must be for commercial/business use
10. **Equipment located outside Canada** — all assets must be in Canada (rolling stock can temporarily cross into USA)

**Why This Matters:** If a customer has no tax number, you may need a **business use certificate** to confirm the equipment isn't for personal use. If you even suspect a prohibited connection, stop the deal immediately.

## 3. CONSTRUCTION — DEEP DIVE

> **This is one of your primary segments.** Know this section cold.

### Target Customer Profile

- **Who:** Residential and commercial construction companies, roadbuilding operations, anyone using standard yellow iron machinery
- **Preferred Credit:** CR4 or better (note: Construction accepts CR4, unlike many other segments that want CR3+)
- **Rating Method:** Credit Scorecard or BTA

### Auto-Decision Limits

| Credit Rating | Auto-Decision Limit |
| --- | --- |
| CR1 / CR2 | **$375,000** |
| CR3 | **$200,000** |
| CR4 | **$0** (every deal requires manual review) |

Subject to Japan Approval Authority for larger amounts.

### Credit Requirements by Exposure Level

**Under $50,000:**
- 2 years in business
- CR4 or better
- No comparable credit required
- No derogatory info
- PG required

**$50,000 to $500,000 (App Only):**
- 3 years in business, active on Equifax/PayNet
- CR4 or better
- Comparable credit IS required (50%/75%/100% rule)
- PG + Signed PNW required for CR3/CR4 or if no comparable credit
- PG must score **above 650 CRP**
- Bank statements/FS required for amounts over $250K if no CC

**$500,000 to $1,000,000:**
- All above criteria PLUS:
- Bank statements may be required
- FS: 3 years accountant-prepared + interim FS if more than 6 months from fiscal year-end
- Ratios, income, and trending must ALL be positive

**Over $1,000,000:**
- **Structured Credit Application** required
- BTA Rating, AR/AP, reviewed FS (best effort)
- Work contracts or detailed projections
- Borrowing base, org chart

### Equipment Tiers — Construction

**Tier 1 (Premium — Best Terms):**
Deere, CAT, Volvo, Komatsu, Hitachi, CASE/New Holland, JCB, Kubota, Liebherr, Terex, Bell, Mitsubishi, Bobcat, Genie, JLG/SkyTrak, Gehl, Manitou, Manitowoc, Grove, Tadano, Link-Belt, American, Yanmar

**Tier 2 (Mid-Market):**
Sany, Hyundai, Kobelco, Doosan, Merlo, Sandvik, Wacker Neuson, Weidemann, Mahindra, Takeuchi, Bomag, Dynapac, Wirtgen/Hamm, Vermeer, Toyota, Crown, Hyster-Yale, Raymond, Clark, Schaffer, Sellick, MEC, Snorkel, Skyjack, Ingersoll-Rand, Tennant, AtlasCopco, Ammann, Sakai, Husqvarna, Multiquip, Stark, Toro, GMH, Haulotte, Ditch Witch

**Tier 3 (Overseas/Niche — Most Restricted):**
Liugong, Zoomlion, XCMG, Sinomach-hi, SDLG (Shandong Lingong), Mecalac, Magni, Dieci, Faresin, Avant Tecno, Green Machine, EcoLift, LiftKing, Load Lifter, Epiroc, all other brands

### Maximum Asset Age — Construction

| Tier | Max Model Years | Max Hours/Year | Absolute Max Hours |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Tier 1 & 2** | **15 model years** | 1,500/year | **15,000 total** |
| **Tier 3** | **10 model years** | 1,500/year | **10,000 total** |

**Example:** A 2015 CAT excavator (Tier 1) in 2025 = 10 model years old. With 12,000 hours, it's still eligible (under 15,000 max). But if it had 16,000 hours, it would be ineligible regardless of age.

### Deal Structure — Construction

| Parameter | Details |
| --- | --- |
| **Max Advance** | 100% of invoice cost |
| **Min Term** | 24 months |
| **Max Term** | 72 months |
| **Attachments** | Over 20% must be itemized/serialized on invoice |
| **Soft Costs** | Less than 10% of equipment cost |

### Down Payment Requirements — Construction

| Credit Rating | Down Payment |
| --- | --- |
| CR1 / CR2 | First payment only |
| CR3 | **5% to 10%** (prefer higher end = 10%) |
| CR4 | **10% to 20%** (prefer higher end = 20%) |

**Key Rule:** The preferred structure is always the **higher end** of the bracket. So for CR3, push for 10%. For CR4, push for 20%.

### Maximum Amortization — Construction

**USED Equipment:** Lower of 72 months OR refer to Age Term Matrix

**NEW Equipment:**

| Tier | CR1 / CR2 / CR3 | CR4 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Tier 1 & 2** | **96 months** | 72 months |
| **Tier 3** | **84 months** | 60 months |

**"Older" New Units (2023 models):**
- Must have an invoice stating the unit is new
- CR1/CR2: Can get like-new terms and conditions
- CR3/CR4: Must deduct at least **12 months of amortization**

### Seasonal Skip Payments — Construction

- **Max 4 months IOP** (not skips)
- IOPs NOT permitted in the first 6 months unless program-approved or offset by equivalent upfront payment
- Preferred for: seasonal businesses with no income during IOP months
- IOP must NOT extend the amortization

### 3rd Party Rental House / DORF — Construction

**CURRENTLY RESTRICTED ABOVE $250,000**
- Only applicable for MHCC reps — **NO broker rental house transactions**
- VP of Credit required for any exceptions
- Requires: FS, PG, regular rental audits, broad form registration, PMSI notices, assignment of lease, doomsday letter, rental floorplan insurance
- Cross-border or rent-to-rental-house end-users are **NOT permitted**
- 3rd party rental contracts must be reviewed/pre-approved by Credit
- No purchase options permitted in rental contracts

### RAC Exceptions — Construction

- **CR1/CR2:** Analysts CAN make exceptions if justification is documented in VISION comments. Can extend max amort by up to 12 months for **USED equipment only** (exceptional basis, strong arguments required)
- **CR3/CR4:** **Next Level Approval Authority required** (no analyst exceptions)
- Credit Manager/Leader and above can approve their own transactions with RAC exceptions

## 4. CONSTRUCTION — ZAXIS PROGRAM

The ZAXIS program is a **special branded program** specifically for Hitachi excavators and wheel loaders sold through **Wajax** (a major Canadian equipment dealer).

### What Makes ZAXIS Different from Standard Construction?

| Feature | Standard Construction | ZAXIS |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Equipment | Any construction OEM | **Hitachi branded only** (via Wajax) |
| Subsidized Rates | No | **Yes** — CR3+ may qualify for 0% subsidized rates |
| App-Only Limit | Varies | **Up to $650,000** |
| Vendor | Multiple | **Wajax Limited** (ZAXIS Financial Services Americas as manufacturer) |
| Max Term | 72 months | **96 months** |
| Oil & Gas | No restriction stated | **Capped at $1MM USD per Japan Policy** |

### Target Customer
- Standard excavation companies
- CR3 or better for subsidized rates (refer to specific model details)
- Oil & Gas operations capped at $1MM USD

### Auto-Decision: Same as standard construction (375K CR1/CR2, 200K CR3, $0 CR4)

### Credit Requirements

**Under $650,000 (App Only):**
- 3 years in business, active on Equifax/PayNet
- CR3 or better, comparable credit preferred
- Contract types: Lease, Loan, FPO, FMV
- Preferably all new customers documented on **MLA** (Master Lease Agreement)
- PG + PNW if no comparable credit. PG above 650 CRP

**$650,000 to $1,300,000:**
- FS: 3 years accountant-prepared + interim
- Exception for CR1/CR2: App only up to $1MM for well-established businesses with strong CC

**Over $1,300,000:**
- Full Structured Credit Application (4Block, spreads, BTA, AR/AP, etc.)

**ZAXIS CC thresholds are more generous:**
- 50% CC for under $150,000
- 75% CC for under $350,000
- 100% CC for over $500,000

### Eligible Assets — ZAXIS

**New & Demo only** — standard Hitachi branded equipment:
- **Compact Excavators:** ZX17 to ZX60
- **Medium & Large Excavators:** ZX75 to ZX890
- **Wheel Loaders:** ZW30 to ZW550

Wajax is vendor of record and can be utilized in repossession events.

### Down Payment — ZAXIS
- CR1 to CR3: **No advance payments required**
- CR4: 10% down payment

### Max Term: 96 months
### Max Amortization: Refer to ZAXIS Finance Program conditions based on model number

<a name="forestry"></a>
## 5. FORESTRY — DEEP DIVE

> **This is one of your primary segments.** Forestry is considered higher risk due to equipment depreciation, harsh operating conditions, and seasonal cash flows.

### Target Customer Profile

- **Who:** Harvest operations, road builders, forestry transport, silviculture (tree planting/maintenance)
- **Preferred Credit:** CR3 or better (average to high credit profile)
- **Rating Method:** Credit Scorecard or BTA

### Auto-Decision: **$0 for all credit ratings**

This means **every single forestry deal** goes through manual review. No auto-approvals. This is because forestry equipment has low resale value, short life expectancy, and the industry is inherently risky.

### Credit Requirements by Exposure Level

**Under $250,000 (App Only):**
- 3+ years in business
- CR3 or better
- Comparable credit required on Equifax/PayNet
- PG required for CR3/CR4 + Signed PNW
- PG must score **above 680** (note: higher than the 650 most other segments require!)
- Bank statements/FS required if no comparable credit
- **Job letter required** with:
  - Number of shifts per day
  - Annual wood production
  - Wood price in $/M3

**$250,000 to $650,000:**
- All above criteria PLUS:
- PG + Signed PNW with info on overall character of the client:
  - Years of experience logging
  - Dealer/customer relationship (new or current, and for how long)
- Bank statements/FS **required for all customer exposure over $250K — NO exceptions**

**$650,000 to $1,000,000:**
- All above PLUS:
- **7 years in business** (longer than any other segment at this level!)
- Active on Equifax/PayNet, no derogatory info
- PG must score **above 725 CRP** (very high threshold)
- FS: 3 years accountant-prepared + interim if more than 6 months from FYE
- Ratios, income, and trending must ALL be positive

**Over $1,000,000:**
- Structured Credit Application
- Spreads / 4Block preparation, BTA rating
- AR/AP, reviewed FS (best effort)
- Work contracts or detailed projections
- Borrowing base, org chart

### Equipment Tiers — Forestry

**Tier 1 (Premium):**
John Deere, Komatsu Forest/Valmet, Tigercat, Ponsse

**Tier 2 (Mid-Market):**
Weiler, TimberPro, Logset, Rottne, Ecolog, Sampo Rosenlew, Liebherr, Link-Belt, Hitachi

**Tier 3 (Other):**
Madill, Eltec, Neuson Forest, HSM, Barko, Serco, Sennebogen, Tanguay, Landrich, all other brands

**CRITICAL RULE:** For **CR3 customers**, only **Tier 1 & Tier 2** equipment is eligible. No Tier 3 for CR3.

### Maximum Asset Age — Forestry

| Metric | Limit |
| --- | --- |
| **Max Model Years** | **8 model years** |
| **Hours per year (1 shift)** | 2,000 hours |
| **Hours per year (2 shifts)** | 4,000 hours |
| **Absolute Max Hours** | **20,000 hours** |

**Why this is different from Construction:** Forestry equipment lives a much harder life. Construction gear might do 1,500 hours/year; forestry gear doing 2 shifts does 4,000 hours/year. The 8-year max is tight because these machines get beaten up in the bush.

### Deal Structure — Forestry

| Parameter | Details |
| --- | --- |
| **Max Advance** | 100% of invoice cost |
| **Max Term (New)** | **60 months** |
| **Max Term (Used)** | **60 months** |
| **Amortization target (2-shift new)** | **48-54 months** |
| **Attachments** | Over 20% must be itemized/serialized |
| **Soft Costs** | Less than 10% of equipment cost |

**Key Insight:** Even though max is 60 months, if the equipment will run 2 shifts, the **target amortization should be 48-54 months** on a new unit. This is because double-shift equipment depreciates much faster.

### Down Payment Requirements — Forestry

| Credit Rating | Down Payment |
| --- | --- |
| CR1 / CR2 | Credit discretion |
| CR3 | **10% to 15%** |
| CR4 | **20%+ with dealer support** |

### Seasonal Skip Payments — Forestry

- **IOP only** (no skip payments)
- **Max 2 months** (April and May — spring breakup season when logging roads are impassable)
- IOPs NOT permitted in first 6 months unless program-approved or offset by equivalent upfront payment
- IOP must NOT extend the amortization

### ITEC2000 Program

The **ITEC2000** program is a special dealer program where transactions come through the ITEC2000 dealer network. Key benefit:
- There is a **dealer UNL (Unlimited) guarantee** in place, adding additional comfort
- This creates an opportunity to take **additional risk** on deals like:
  - CR4 customers
  - Start-up businesses
- The dealer acknowledges the risk (they have skin in the game)

**Why This Matters:** If a deal comes through ITEC2000 and the customer is CR4 or even a startup, you might still be able to do it because the dealer is backing it. Without ITEC2000, a CR4 forestry deal with a startup would be nearly impossible.

### RAC Exceptions — Forestry

**THIS IS THE STRICTEST EXCEPTION POLICY IN THE RAC:**
- **Analysts have NO exception authority whatsoever** (unlike Construction where analysts can make CR1/CR2 exceptions)
- **Next Level Approval Authority required** for any exception
- Credit Manager/Leader and above can approve their own transactions with RAC exceptions

### 3rd Party Rental House / DORF: **N/A** (not applicable in Forestry)

## 6. TRANSPORTATION — CLASS 1-7 (Deep Dive)

> **This is one of your primary segments.** Class 1-7 covers everything from pickup trucks to medium-duty specialized vehicles.

### What Are Classes 1-7?

Vehicle classes are defined by **Gross Vehicle Weight Rating (GVWR)**:
- **Class 1-3:** Light duty — pickups, cargo vans (under 14,000 lbs)
- **Class 4-5:** Medium duty — delivery trucks, small buses (14,001-19,500 lbs)
- **Class 6-7:** Medium-heavy duty — larger trucks, school buses, garbage trucks (19,501-33,000 lbs)

### Target Customer Profile

- **Who:** Specialized, medium, and light duty transport operations used for a variety of functions
- **Preferred Credit:** CR3 or better
- **CRITICAL RULE:** **No first-time owner/operators, regardless of the situation** — this is a hard rule with no exceptions
- **Rating Method:** Credit Scorecard or BTA

### Auto-Decision Limits

| Credit Rating | Auto-Decision Limit |
| --- | --- |
| CR1 / CR2 | **$375,000** |
| CR3 | **$200,000** |
| CR4 | **$0** |

### Credit Requirements by Exposure Level

**$0 to $250,000 (App Only):**
- 3 years in business (or equivalent business ownership via sole proprietorship)
- Active on commercial Equifax/PayNet matching TIB (time in business)
- Comparable credit required
- PG + Signed PNW for CR3/CR4 or if no comparable credit
- PG above 650 CRP
- Work letter from known carrier **may be required**
- FS required for amounts over $250K if no CC

**$250,000 to $500,000:**
- Above criteria PLUS:
- FS: 3 years accountant-prepared + interim if more than 6 months from FYE
- 3 most recent months of company bank statements may be required
- Ratios, income, and trending must ALL be positive

**$500,000 to $1,000,000:**
- Credit discretion
- **Call to verify work program** for all transactions over $650,000

**Over $1,000,000:**
- Structured Credit Application (BTA, 4Block spreads, AR/AP, reviewed FS, detailed projections, borrowing base, org chart)

### Eligible Assets — Class 1-7

| Asset Class | Examples |
| --- | --- |
| **Pickups / Cargo Vans** | Ford F-Series, RAM, Chevy Silverado, Transit, Sprinter |
| **Medium Duty** | Hino, Isuzu NPR, Ford F-650 |
| **Specialized / Vocational** | Fuel trucks, garbage trucks, cement trucks, crane trucks, tow trucks, dump trucks |
| **Buses** | School buses, coach buses (coach = exception only, reviewed by Manager) |
| **Trailers** | Grain, live bottom, lowboys, dump trailers, tank trailers |

**Important Rules:**
- All buses & hazmat transactions must be treated as a **LOAN** (not lease) with increased insurance
- School bus transactions should be directly tied to a **contract with the local school board**
- Coach buses: **exception basis only**, reviewed by Manager

### Maximum Asset Age — Class 1-7

| Asset Type | Max Model Years | Max KMs/Year | Max Total KMs |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Pickups / Cargo** | 8 years | 50,000 | 250,000 |
| **Medium Duty** | 10 years | 80,000 | 400,000 |
| **Buses** | 10 years | 50,000 (school) / 200,000 (coach) | Varies |
| **Specialized / Vocational** | 15 years | 30,000-50,000 (engine), 1,500 (crane attachment) | Varies |
| **Trailers** | 15 years | N/A | N/A |
| **Dump Trucks** | 15 years (max 24 months after 10 years) | 50,000 | 500,000 |

### Down Payment Requirements — Class 1-7

| Credit Rating | Down Payment |
| --- | --- |
| CR1 / CR2 | Credit discretion |
| CR3 | **5% to 10%** (prefer 10%) |
| CR4 | **10% to 20%** (prefer 20%) |
| CR5 / CCC+ & lower | Case by case |

### Term & Amortization — Class 1-7

**Max Term:** 72 months — but for every model year (or when max KMs per asset class is hit), **deduct 12 months**

**Example:** A 3-year-old pickup truck: 72 - (3 × 12) = **36 months max term**

**Max Amortization (NEW):**

| Credit Rating | Max Amort |
| --- | --- |
| CR1 / CR2 / CR3 | **84 months** |
| CR4 | **60 months** |

**Used:** Lower of 72 months or refer to Age Term Matrix

### Seasonal Skip Payments — Class 1-7
- Max 4 months IOP (no skips)
- Same rules as Construction — not in first 6 months, must not extend amortization

### Personal Guarantee — Class 1-7
- **REQUIRED for CR3 to CR5**
- Signed PNW must be included
- **Homeownership must be verified prior to funding**
- At credit discretion for CR1/CR2

### Route One Program (NEW ONLY)

Route One is a specific program within Transportation Class 1-7:

| Credit Rating | Max Term | Down Payment |
| --- | --- | --- |
| CR1 | 72 months | First payment only |
| CR2 | 72 months | First payment only |
| CR3 | 60 months | 5% or First & Last to 10% |
| CR4 | 48 months | 10% to 20% |

**Residual Values (Route One):**
- CR1/CR2/CR3: 72 months = 20%, 60 months = 30%, 48 months = 40%, 36 months = 50%, 24 months = 60%
- CR4: Max 60 months, $0 residual

### Electric Buses & Trucks

This is a growing area. Special rules apply:

| Feature | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| **Down Payment** | CR1/CR2: first only. CR3: 5-10%. CR4: 10%+ |
| **Max Amort (Investment Grade)** | **120 months** |
| **Max Amort (Standard CR1-CR3)** | 84 months |
| **Max Amort (Young Business / CR4)** | 72 months (bus only) |
| **Product Type** | Trucks = Lease or Loan. Buses = **Loan only** |
| **Tier 1 OEMs** | Ford, GM, Volvo, BlueBird |
| **Tier 2 OEMs (max 60 months)** | Workhorse, Battlemotors, Nikola |

**Government Grants:** Many EV transactions include federal/provincial grants:
- Federal: iMHZEV, iZEV, Investment Bank of Canada
- Provincial: Clean BC Go Electric, Ecocamionnage (QC), Plug In NB, EV Assist Nova Scotia
- Transactions involving Investment Bank of Canada (BIC) require an **intercreditor agreement**

**Key Questions to Ask for EV Deals:**
1. Why purchase EV instead of diesel?
2. How and where will they charge the unit(s)?
3. Does MHCCA have a relationship with the vendor or manufacturer?

## 7. TRANSPORTATION — CLASS 8 OTR (Deep Dive)

> **This is one of your primary segments.** Class 8 is the big-rig segment — over-the-road freight. This is the **highest risk** transportation segment.

### What Is Class 8?

Class 8 = vehicles with GVWR over 33,000 lbs. Think: 18-wheelers, long-haul trucks, highway tractors.

### Target Customer Profile

- **Who:** Established fleet customers doing over-the-road freight (general freight operations)
- **Risk Note:** Dry van/reefer hauling is **higher risk** compared to flat deck or western oil-related heavy haul
- **Preferred Credit:** CR3 or better
- **Must have:** Clear yard and office location, easily viewable online or via site visit
- **CRITICAL RULES:**
  - **No first-time owner/operators, regardless of the situation**
  - **ALL borrowers must be a verified homeowner** to even be considered

### Auto-Decision: **N/A** — There are NO auto-decisions in Class 8

Every single Class 8 deal gets manually reviewed. This is the only transportation segment with zero auto-decision authority.

### Credit Requirements by Exposure Level

**$0 to $250,000 (App Only):**
- **5 years in business** (note: longer than Class 1-7 which only requires 3 years)
- Active on commercial Equifax/PayNet matching TIB
- Comparable credit required
- PG + Signed PNW for CR3/CR4 or if no comparable credit
- PG above 650 CRP
- Work letter from known carrier may be required
- FS required for amounts over $250K if no CC

**$250,000 to $500,000:**
- Above criteria PLUS:
- FS: 3 years accountant-prepared + interim
- Bank statements may be required
- **Preferred CR3 or better**
- Ratios, income, and trending must ALL be positive

**$500,000 to $1,000,000:**
- Credit discretion
- Call to verify work program for all transactions over $650,000
- **Preferred CR2 or better** (note: stricter than Class 1-7)

**Over $1,000,000:**
- Full Structured Credit Application (BTA, 4Block, AR/AP, FS, projections, borrowing base, org chart)

### Equipment Tiers — Class 8

| Tier | Brands |
| --- | --- |
| **Tier 1** | **Kenworth, Peterbilt** |
| **Tier 2** | **Western Star, MACK** |
| **Tier 3** | **Volvo, Freightliner, International** |

**Important:** Note that Volvo and Freightliner — which are very common trucks — are Tier 3 in Class 8 OTR. This is because their resale values in the long-haul market are lower than Kenworth/Peterbilt.

**Eligible Asset Types:** Class 8 Sleeper, Daycab + Dryvan, Reefer, Flatbed

### Maximum Asset Age — Class 8

This is where it gets tricky because the rules differ by **geography**:

**ONTARIO & WESTERN PROVINCES:**

| Credit Rating | New | Used |
| --- | --- | --- |
| CR1 | Yes | **7 years max vintage, max 700,000 km at funding** |
| CR2 | Yes | **7 years max vintage, max 700,000 km at funding** |
| CR3 | Yes | **3-year countback** from current year only |
| CR4 / CCC+ & lower | **Not eligible for new** | **NOT ELIGIBLE AT ALL** |

**QUEBEC & MARITIMES:**

| Credit Rating | New | Used |
| --- | --- | --- |
| CR1 | Yes | **7 years max vintage, max 700,000 km at funding** |
| CR2 | Yes | **7 years max vintage, max 700,000 km at funding** |
| CR3 | Yes | **3-year countback** from current year |
| CR4 / CCC+ & lower | **Yes (new)** | **3-year countback** from current year |

**Key Difference:** In Ontario and Western provinces, **CR4 customers are NOT eligible for used Class 8 trucks at all**. In Quebec and the Maritimes, CR4 can get used trucks but only 3-year countback.

### Deal Structure — Class 8

| Parameter | Details |
| --- | --- |
| **Max Advance** | 100% of invoice cost |
| **Max Term** | **60 months** — deduct 12 months per model year or per 200,000 km |
| **Max Amortization** | **60 months** |
| **Soft Costs** | Less than 10% (moose bumpers, warranty, GPS, decals) |

**Example:** A 2-year-old Kenworth with 350,000 km:
- Age: 60 - (2 × 12) = 36 months max
- KMs: 350,000 / 200,000 = 1.75, so deduct 12 months once → 48 months
- Take the LOWER: **36 months max term**

### Down Payment — Class 8

| Credit Rating | Down Payment |
| --- | --- |
| CR1 / CR2 | Analyst discretion |
| CR3 | **10% minimum** |
| CR4 (QC & Maritimes only) | **10% to 15%** |

**CRITICAL:** All down payments must be paid **directly to MHCCA** — no down payments to suppliers will be accepted.

### Remarketing
- All used assets based on **orderly liquidation value or auction value**
- Conservative LTV (loan-to-value) for Tier 2 & 3 brands

### Personal Guarantee — Class 8
- **REQUIRED for CR3 to CR5**
- Signed PNW must be included
- **Homeownership to be verified prior to funding**
- At credit discretion for CR1/CR2

### RAC Exceptions — Class 8

**Same strict policy as Forestry:**
- **Analysts have NO exception authority**
- Next Level Approval Authority required
- Credit Manager/Leader and above can approve their own transactions with exceptions

### 3rd Party Rental House / DORF: **N/A**

<a name="rac-exceptions"></a>
## 21. RAC EXCEPTIONS — HOW THEY WORK

RAC exceptions follow a consistent hierarchy across most segments, but there are important differences:

### Standard Exception Policy (Used by: Construction, Agriculture, Industrial, Manufacturing, Lawn & Garden, Small Ticket, Transport Class 1-7)

- **CR1/CR2:** Analysts CAN make exceptions — must document justification in VISION comments. Can extend max amort by up to 12 months for **USED equipment only** (exceptional basis, strong arguments required)
- **CR3/CR4:** Next Level Approval Authority required
- Credit Manager/Leader and above can self-approve RAC exceptions

### Strict Exception Policy (Used by: Forestry, Transport Class 8, SDG-Energy, Hospitality)

- **Analysts have ZERO exception authority**
- **All exceptions** require Next Level Approval Authority
- Credit Manager/Leader and above can self-approve

### Stricter Exception Policy (Used by: Office Equipment, Technology, Healthcare)

- Next Level Approval Authority required
- **Senior Manager and above** (not just Credit Manager) can self-approve

<a name="practice-questions"></a>
## 23. PRACTICE QUESTIONS & ANSWERS

### Construction Questions

**Q1: A construction company rated CR3 wants to finance a brand-new CAT excavator for $300,000. Can this be auto-approved?**

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

No, it cannot be auto-approved because the auto-decision limit for CR3 is $200,000, and this deal is $300,000. It will need to go to manual review by a credit analyst or manager.

</details>

**Q2: What is the maximum amortization for a new Tier 1 construction asset for a CR2 customer?**

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**96 months.** For new Tier 1 & Tier 2 construction equipment, CR1/CR2/CR3 customers can get up to 96 months amortization.

</details>

**Q3: A CR4 customer wants to finance a new Tier 3 (Liugong) excavator. What is the max amortization?**

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**60 months.** For new Tier 3 construction equipment, CR4 customers are limited to 60 months. (CR1-CR3 on Tier 3 would be 84 months.)

</details>

**Q4: A Tier 2 construction asset is 11 years old with 14,000 hours. Is it eligible? If so, what's the max amort?**

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

Yes, it's eligible. Tier 1 & 2 max is 15 model years and 15,000 hours. At 11 years old, per the Age Term Matrix, max amortization is **48 months**. The 14,000 hours is under the 15,000 max.

</details>

**Q5: Can a broker submit a $300,000 rental house deal in the Construction segment?**

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

No. Rental house transactions above $250,000 are currently restricted, AND they are only applicable for MHCC reps — **no broker rental house transactions** at all. VP of Credit required for any exceptions.

</details>

**Q6: What down payment should you request for a CR3 construction deal?**

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**5% to 10%, with a preference for the higher end (10%).** The RAC states the preferred structure is always the higher end of the bracket.

</details>

### Forestry Questions

**Q7: What is the auto-decision limit for forestry deals?**

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**$0 for all credit ratings.** Every forestry deal requires manual review. There are no auto-approvals in forestry.

</details>

**Q8: A CR3 customer wants to finance a Tier 3 (Barko) forestry processor. Can we do it?**

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**No.** For CR3 customers in forestry, only **Tier 1 and Tier 2** equipment is eligible. Tier 3 is not available for CR3. The customer would need to be CR1 or CR2 to finance Tier 3 forestry equipment.

</details>

**Q9: What is the maximum asset age for forestry equipment?**

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**8 model years**, with hours not to exceed 20,000. For single-shift operations, that's 2,000 hours/year; for double-shift, 4,000 hours/year.

</details>

**Q10: A forestry customer wants to finance $500,000 worth of Tigercat equipment. What credit requirements apply?**

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

Since exposure is $250K-$650K, they need: all the base criteria PLUS PG + Signed PNW with info on character (years of logging experience, dealer/customer relationship), and bank statements/FS are **required — no exceptions** for exposure over $250K.

</details>

**Q11: Can an analyst approve a RAC exception for a CR1 forestry deal?**

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**No.** Forestry has the strictest exception policy — analysts have **zero exception authority** regardless of credit rating. All exceptions require Next Level Approval Authority. This is different from Construction where analysts can make CR1/CR2 exceptions.

</details>

**Q12: What information must be included in the job letter for forestry applications?**

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

Three things: (1) Number of shifts per day, (2) Annual wood production, (3) Wood price in $/M3.

</details>

**Q13: A forestry deal is for $800,000. What minimum PG score is needed?**

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**725 CRP.** For forestry exposure of $650K-$1MM, the PG must score above 725 (the highest threshold of any segment at this level). The customer also needs 7+ years in business.

</details>

**Q14: What is the ITEC2000 program and how does it help with risky forestry deals?**

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

ITEC2000 is a dealer program where the dealer provides an unlimited guarantee (UNL), adding comfort. This allows MHCCA to take additional risk on transactions that would normally be declined, such as CR4 customers or startups. The dealer acknowledges the risk and has skin in the game.

</details>

### Transportation Questions

**Q15: A brand-new trucking company (1 year in business) with an excellent personal credit score wants to buy their first Kenworth. Can we finance it?**

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**No.** Both Class 1-7 and Class 8 have a hard rule: **No first-time owner/operators, regardless of the situation.** The personal credit score doesn't matter — the rule is absolute.

</details>

**Q16: What are the Tier 1 OEMs for Class 8 OTR?**

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**Kenworth and Peterbilt** — only two brands. Tier 2 is Western Star and MACK. Tier 3 is Volvo, Freightliner, and International.

</details>

**Q17: Can a CR4 customer in Ontario finance a used Class 8 truck?**

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**No.** In Ontario and Western provinces, CR4 customers are **not eligible** for used Class 8 trucks at all. However, in Quebec and the Maritimes, CR4 can get used trucks with a 3-year countback.

</details>

**Q18: A Class 8 truck is 4 years old with 500,000 km. What is the maximum term?**

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

Calculate both:
- By age: 60 - (4 × 12) = 12 months
- By KMs: 500,000 / 200,000 = 2.5 → deduct 24 months → 60 - 24 = 36 months
Take the **lower**: **12 months** max term. This deal would barely work.

</details>

**Q19: What makes Class 8 auto-decision different from all other transportation segments?**

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

Class 8 has **no auto-decision at all (N/A)**. Every single deal is manually reviewed. This is the only segment where even CR1 customers don't get auto-approval. Class 1-7, by contrast, has 375K for CR1/CR2.

</details>

**Q20: What special requirement applies to ALL Class 8 borrowers that doesn't apply to Class 1-7?**

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

All Class 8 borrowers must be a **verified homeowner** to be considered. This applies to every credit rating. Additionally, Class 8 requires 5 years in business (vs 3 years for Class 1-7).

</details>

**Q21: What is the maximum amortization for an Investment Grade electric bus in the Class 1-7 segment?**

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**120 months.** For investment-grade borrowers, electric buses and trucks can get up to 120 months. Standard CR1-CR3 profiles get 84 months. Young businesses/CR4 get 72 months (bus only).

</details>

**Q22: For Class 1-7, what is the maximum amortization for new equipment for a CR4 customer?**

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**60 months.** CR1/CR2/CR3 get 84 months max amort on new equipment, while CR4 is capped at 60 months.

</details>

### Cross-Segment Questions

**Q23: Which segments do NOT allow CR5 customers at all?**

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**SDG-Energy, Technology, and Office Equipment** explicitly state CR5 is not permitted. Healthcare also doesn't apply CR5 ratings.

</details>

**Q24: Which segments have ZERO analyst exception authority?**

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**Forestry, Transportation Class 8 OTR, SDG-Energy, and Hospitality.** All exceptions require Next Level Approval Authority.

</details>

**Q25: What is Comparable Credit and what are the three thresholds?**

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

Comparable Credit means the customer has existing credit history similar in size to what they're requesting. The thresholds are:
- Under $50K: 50% comparable credit
- Under $100K: 75% comparable credit
- $100K and above: 100% comparable credit

</details>

**Q26: Name three things that are absolutely prohibited under MHCCA's restricted industries policy.**

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

Any three of: weapons manufacturing/sales, organized crime, cryptocurrency, cannabis, gaming/gambling, usury/money laundering, piracy/trademark violations, obscene materials, consumer/personal use, equipment outside Canada, Oil & Gas transactions above $1MM USD.

</details>

**Q27: A CR2 analyst wants to self-approve a 12-month amortization extension on a used construction asset. Can they?**

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**Yes.** In Construction, analysts are authorized to extend max amort by up to 12 months for CR1/CR2 on used equipment only, provided they document strong justification in VISION comments. This is an exceptional basis.

</details>

**Q28: What is the difference between IOP and skip payments?**

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

An IOP (Interim Operating Period) is structured at the start of the deal for seasonal businesses — the customer doesn't make payments during specific months (e.g., winter for construction). Skip payments are ad hoc pauses. Most RACs allow IOP but NOT skips. IOPs cannot be in the first 6 months and must not extend the amortization.

</details>

**Q29: What does DORF stand for and why is it restricted?**

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

DORF = Dealer Owned Rental Fleet. It's when a dealer finances equipment through MHCCA specifically to rent it out to end-users. It's restricted (above $250K, MHCC reps only, no brokers) because: the end-user isn't our customer, rental equipment gets more abuse, and recovery is harder in a default. VP of Credit required for exceptions.

</details>

**Q30: An agriculture customer wants to finance a new grain bin. What special requirements apply?**

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

Non-standard AG assets like grain bins require: CR1/CR2 only (superior credit quality), the borrower must own the land (title search required), must be a Loan & Security Agreement, max amort 120 months, PG beacon score 700+, and fixture filings/landlord waivers for exposures over $250K.

</details>
