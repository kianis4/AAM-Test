# STUDY GUIDE: Driver's License Validation Guidelines

### *MHCCA Internal Training Guide — June 2025*

---

## WHAT IS THIS DOCUMENT ABOUT?

This guide covers **how to validate Canadian driver's licenses** as part of MHCCA's identity verification process. Every lease contract requires valid identification from the signatory, and driver's licenses are the most common form of ID presented. But not all driver's licenses are created equal — each province has different formats, security features, and license number structures.

Think of it this way: if someone hands you a Quebec driver's license, you need to know that the license number encodes the person's birthdate and last name, that the expiration date always falls on their birthday, and that the ink shifts from gold to green when you tilt it. If any of those things don't match, you might be looking at a fraudulent ID — and financing equipment based on a fake identity is a fast track to a loss.

**Why should you care?** Both the **Operations Team** and the **Sales Team** are responsible for validating driver's licenses. If you approve a deal with a fraudulent ID, MHCCA has financed equipment for someone who may not exist or may not be who they claim to be. Know the security features and license number formats for each province.

---

## SECTION 1: ONTARIO

### License Number Format

**Example:** D6101-40706-60905

| Element | What It Means |
|---|---|
| **First character** | First letter of the **last name** |
| **Last 6 digits** | Represent the **birthdate** in format YYMMDD |
| **Females** | Add **50** to the month (MM) portion |

**Example decoded:** D6101-40706-60905 → Last name starts with "D", born 1966/09/05 (last 6 digits: 660905... but with Ontario's specific encoding in the middle digits)

### Security Features to Check

| Feature | What to Look For |
|---|---|
| **Ontario logos** | Touch the boundary of the photo |
| **Font** | Bold font on driver's license number and date of birth |
| **Laser-engraved signature** | Signature and photo visible in scan |
| **Photo** | Clear and matches the person |
| **Signature** | Matches across the card |

---

## SECTION 2: BRITISH COLUMBIA

### License Number Format

**7 or 8-digit alphanumeric** code (8-digit since July 2023)

**Example:** DL:1234562

### Security Features to Check

| Feature | What to Look For |
|---|---|
| **Front photo** | Black and white (grey scale) on front left |
| **Names** | Etched, raised — can be **felt by rubbing your finger** over them |
| **Signature** | Etched, raised, overlaid on primary cardholder picture |
| **Microprinting** | Line of microprint below signature (issued Feb 2020 – Feb 2022) |
| **Rainbow printing** | On both sides of card; background design overlaid on edges of photo |
| **Secondary "ghosted" picture** | Shows cardholder picture and year of birth, shifts when tilted |
| **Restrictions/endorsements** | Codes listed on front, described on back |

### BC Card Evolution by Issue Date

| Period | Feature Changes |
|---|---|
| **Feb 2020 – Feb 2022** | Line of microprint below signature; secondary picture has BC flag overlapping lower left corner |
| **After Feb 2022** | Secondary picture includes full DOB and signature, shifts when tilted |

---

## SECTION 3: ALBERTA

### License Number Format

**Numeric** (e.g., 134711-320)

### Security Features to Check

| Feature | What to Look For |
|---|---|
| **Photo** | High-definition, laser-engraved over printed background with fine lines running through |
| **Rainbow printing** | Gradual colour transition — difficult to scan and replicate |
| **Three transparent windows** | One Alberta-shaped (smaller photo), two maple leaf-shaped |
| **Raised text** | License number, DOB, expiry, and signature engraved into card core — difficult to alter/replicate |
| **Clear laser engraving** | Feel the clear lettering (DOB and sex) to the left of the photo |
| **Alberta shield** | Embossed, can be seen and felt in lower part of clear window |
| **Surface embossing** | Fine line pattern covering front; includes Alberta signature, provincial shield, seal of Registrar of Motor Vehicle Services, wheat graphics |
| **Albertosaurus image** | Laser-engraved dinosaur, precise alignment with Alberta window, tail travels front-to-back, raised above card surface with 3D effect |
| **"Ghost" DOB** | Security background visible through characters of birth month and year |

> **Why This Matters:** Alberta has the most security features of any province. The Albertosaurus image and the three transparent windows are particularly hard to fake. If someone presents an Alberta license without these features, it's likely fraudulent.

## SECTION 4: QUEBEC

### License Number Format

**Produced as of July 13, 2023**

**Format:** A####DDMMYY##

| Component | What It Represents |
|---|---|
| **A** | First letter of **last name** |
| **####** | Four digits encoding the last name |
| **DD** | Day of birth |
| **MM** | Month of birth |
| **YY** | Last two digits of birth year |
| **##** | Two check digits |

**Example:** L1531-171274-08 → Last name starts with "L", born December 17, 1974

### Critical Quebec-Specific Rules

- All labels and fields are **in French** as per Quebec standards
- The **expiration date corresponds to the date of birth** (renews on birthday)
- The license is called "Permis de conduire"

### Security Features to Check

| Feature | What to Look For |
|---|---|
| **Photo** | High-definition black and white with wavy lines running through it |
| **Tactile engraving** | Particularly on date of birth — you can feel it |
| **Optically variable ink** | Shifts from **gold to green** depending on angle and lighting, especially at the **fleur-de-lys** level |
| **2D barcode (back)** | Contains all license info EXCEPT photo and signature |
| **Bar code (back)** | Corresponds to the driver's license number |

### Quebec License Fields

| Field # | Content |
|---|---|
| 4d | Driver's license number |
| 1 | Last name |
| 2 | First name(s) |
| 3 | Date of birth |
| 8 | Licence holder's address |
| 9 | Driver's license class(es) |
| 15 | Sex |
| 12 | Condition(s) |
| 16 | Height (cm) |
| 18 | Eye colour |
| 9a | Endorsement(s) |
| 5 | Reference number |
| 4b | Date of expiry |
| 4a | Date of issue |

> **Why This Matters:** The Quebec license number literally encodes the person's last name initial and full birthdate. This means you can **cross-verify** the license number against the person's stated name and DOB. If the license says "L1531-171274-08" but the person says their birthday is March 5, the license number doesn't match — red flag.

## SECTION 5: MANITOBA

### License Number Format

**Format:** Letters followed by alphanumeric code

| Position | What It Represents |
|---|---|
| **First 5 letters** | First 5 letters of **last name** |
| **6th letter** | First letter of **first name** |
| **7th letter** | First letter of **middle name** |
| **No middle name** | * is used in its place |
| **Last name < 5 letters** | * fills the remaining positions |

**Example:** PU-BL-IJ-Q008NH → Last name starts with "PUBLI" (PUBLIC), first name "J" (JAMES), middle name... etc.

### Security Features to Check

| Feature | What to Look For |
|---|---|
| **Guilloche patterns** | Touch the background of the photo |
| **Signature** | Matches across the card |
| **Laser-engraved signature** | Signature and photo visible in scan |
| **Photo** | Clear and matches the person |

---

## SECTION 6: SASKATCHEWAN

### License Number Format

**8-digit numeric code** (e.g., 20000030)

### Security Features to Check

| Feature | What to Look For |
|---|---|
| **Fine line background** | Behind high-quality photo; tampering creates visible edges |
| **Clear laser feature** | Etched near photo (front) and center (back); contains DOB, gender, customer number |
| **Oval-shaped window** | Secondary photo image with reverse visible from back |
| **Wave text** | Partially visible through window |
| **Tactile data fields** | Raised DOB, expiry date, customer number, and wave text |
| **Laser-engraved signature** | Matches and overlaps the photo |
| **Wavy line** | Variable-size text (DOB and name) partially enters clear window and is tactile |

---

## SECTION 7: NEW BRUNSWICK

### License Number Format

**Unique identifier** (e.g., 105502)

### Document Reference Number (Point 5)

**Format:** NBYYYYMMDDXXXXYYYYMMDD

| Component | What It Represents |
|---|---|
| **NB** + first YYYYMMDD | **Issue date** |
| **XXXX** | Internal code |
| **Last YYYYMMDD** | **Date of birth** |

### Security Features to Check

| Feature | What to Look For |
|---|---|
| **Intricate wavy lines** | Touch the background of the photo |
| **Signature** | Matches across the card |
| **Small photo** | Includes date of birth |
| **Photo** | Clear and matches the person |

---

## SECTION 8: NOVA SCOTIA

### License Number Format (Point 4d)

**Format:** LLLLLDDMMYYXXX

| Component | What It Represents |
|---|---|
| **LLLLL** | First **5 letters of last name** |
| **DDMMYY** | **Date of birth** |
| **XXX** | Internal serial number / unique identifier |

### Security Features to Check

| Feature | What to Look For |
|---|---|
| **Wavy lines** | Greenish-yellow with blue starburst and faint grid-like lines touching photo background |
| **Interlocking circular lines** | Blue floral elements across the license |
| **Green wavy lines** | Complex, repetitive pattern at bottom |
| **Signature** | Matches across the card |
| **Laser-engraved small photo** | With date of birth |
| **Photo** | Clear and matches the person |

---

## SECTION 9: NEWFOUNDLAND AND LABRADOR

### License Number Format (Point 4d) — Two Possible Formats

**Format 1:** AYYMMDD###

| Component | What It Represents |
|---|---|
| **A** | First letter of **last name** |
| **YY** | Last two digits of **birth year** |
| **MM** | Month of birth |
| **DD** | Day of birth |
| **###** | Unique numbers assigned to the license |

**Format 2:** AA#######

| Component | What It Represents |
|---|---|
| **AA** | Any letters |
| **#######** | A sequence of numbers |

### Document Reference Number (Point 5)

**Format:** NLXXXXXXXXXXXXX — NL = province code, rest = system-generated unique identifier

### Security Features to Check

| Feature | What to Look For |
|---|---|
| **Interlocking circular lines** | At top only, with blue floral element |
| **Wavy faint lines** | Touch the background of the photo |
| **Green wavy lines** | Interlocking pattern at bottom |
| **Signature** | Matches across the card |
| **Laser-engraved small photo** | With DOB and heart at top (indicating organ donor status) |
| **Photo** | Clear and matches the person |

## SECTION 10: UNIVERSAL VALIDATION CHECKS — FOR ALL PROVINCES

Both the **Operations Team** and the **Sales Team** should check these 7 items on EVERY driver's license, regardless of province:

| # | Check | What to Look For |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Raised Lettering** | Text (license number, DOB, expiry, signature) should be tactile and slightly raised, engraved into card core for tamper resistance |
| 2 | **Holographic or Optically Variable Elements** | Look for holograms (trillium, fleur-de-lys, Alberta shield); color-shifting ink (gold to green) depending on angle |
| 3 | **Ghost or Secondary Photo** | Present on most cards; may include DOB overlay or signature; should shift or remain static depending on card type |
| 4 | **Tactile Surface Checks** | Feel for engraving, texture, embossing (Alberta's surface embossing, QC's tactile DOB) |
| 5 | **Visual Inspection** | Check for irregular fonts, color inconsistencies, tampering, or damage |
| 6 | **Card Quality** | Confirm polycarbonate material, proper thickness and durability; some cards produce a "tinny" sound when dropped (e.g., older BC cards) |
| 7 | **Barcode & Data Alignment** | Ensure barcodes & magstripes match printed data; check for machine-readable zones (e.g., Enhanced BC cards) |

## SECTION 11: LICENSE NUMBER FORMAT — QUICK REFERENCE TABLE

| Province | Format | Key Feature |
|---|---|---|
| **Ontario** | Starts with first letter of last name; last 6 digits = YYMMDD (+50 for females) | Encodes name + DOB |
| **British Columbia** | 7 or 8-digit alphanumeric (8-digit since July 2023) | Simple numeric |
| **Alberta** | Numeric (e.g., 134711-320) | Numeric only |
| **Quebec** | A####DDMMYY## | Encodes last name initial + full DOB + check digits |
| **Manitoba** | First 5 = last name, 6th = first name initial, 7th = middle name initial | Encodes full name |
| **Saskatchewan** | 8-digit numeric | Simple numeric |
| **New Brunswick** | Unique identifier; Doc ref encodes issue date + DOB | Doc ref has dates |
| **Nova Scotia** | LLLLLDDMMYYXXX (5 letters of last name + DOB + serial) | Encodes name + DOB |
| **Newfoundland** | Format 1: AYYMMDD### or Format 2: AA####### | Two possible formats |

## SECTION 12: CROSS-VERIFICATION TIPS

When checking a driver's license, use the license number to cross-verify against the applicant's information:

| Province | What You Can Verify |
|---|---|
| **Ontario** | First letter of last name + birthdate (YYMMDD in last 6 digits) |
| **Quebec** | First letter of last name + full birthdate (DDMMYY) |
| **Manitoba** | First 5 letters of last name + first name initial + middle name initial |
| **Nova Scotia** | First 5 letters of last name + birthdate (DDMMYY) |
| **Newfoundland (Format 1)** | First letter of last name + birthdate (YYMMDD) |
| **New Brunswick (Doc Ref)** | Issue date + birthdate (both YYYYMMDD) |

If the encoded information in the license number doesn't match the printed information on the card or the client's stated details — **stop and investigate.**

## PRACTICE QUESTIONS & ANSWERS

Test yourself. Cover the answers and try to answer from memory.

---

### Question 1
**An Ontario driver's license number starts with "M." What does this tell you about the cardholder?**

**Answer:** Their **last name starts with the letter M.**

---

### Question 2
**On an Ontario license, the last 6 digits are 950315. What is the cardholder's date of birth? What if the cardholder is female?**

**Answer:** For a male: born **1995/03/15** (March 15, 1995). For a female: the month has 50 added, so 950315 would NOT be a female — a female born March 15, 1995 would have the digits 95**53**15 (03 + 50 = 53).

---

### Question 3
**What is the BC driver's license number format, and what changed in July 2023?**

**Answer:** 7 or 8-digit **alphanumeric** code. Since **July 2023**, BC switched to **8-digit** format.

---

### Question 4
**A Quebec driver's license number is L1531-171274-08. What is the cardholder's birth date, and what letter does their last name start with?**

**Answer:** Last name starts with **"L"**. The DDMMYY portion is 171274, which means born **December 17, 1974** (DD=17, MM=12, YY=74).

---

### Question 5
**On a Quebec license, the expiration date is December 17, 2031. What does this tell you about the cardholder?**

**Answer:** The cardholder's **birthday is December 17** — Quebec licenses expire on the date of birth, and the card states "Paiement exigé chaque année à votre date anniversaire de naissance" (payment required each year on your birthday).

---

### Question 6
**What are the 3 security features unique to Alberta licenses that involve transparent elements?**

**Answer:**
1. **Alberta-shaped transparent window** containing a smaller size photo
2. **Two maple leaf-shaped transparent windows**
3. Photo placement is precise and visible from **both sides** of the card

---

### Question 7
**A Manitoba license number starts with "SM-IT-HJ." What can you determine about the cardholder's name?**

**Answer:**
- Last name starts with **SMITH** (first 5 letters)
- First name starts with **H** (6th letter)
- Middle name starts with **J** (7th letter)

---

### Question 8
**Name the 7 universal validation checks that apply to ALL provincial driver's licenses.**

**Answer:**
1. Raised Lettering
2. Holographic or Optically Variable Elements
3. Ghost or Secondary Photo
4. Tactile Surface Checks
5. Visual Inspection
6. Card Quality
7. Barcode & Data Alignment

---

### Question 9
**Which provinces encode the cardholder's birthdate directly in the license number?**

**Answer:** Ontario, Quebec, Nova Scotia, and Newfoundland (Format 1). Manitoba encodes the name but not the birthdate. New Brunswick encodes the birthdate in the document reference number (Point 5), not the license number itself.

---

### Question 10
**A Nova Scotia license number is LEFEV020220005. Break down each component.**

**Answer:**
- **LEFEV** = First 5 letters of last name (LEFEV... likely LEFEVRE)
- **020220** = DOB in DDMMYY format → **February 2, 1920** (or 2020)
- **005** = Internal serial number / unique identifier

---

### Question 11
**What Quebec license security feature involves ink that changes colour when tilted? What colours does it shift between?**

**Answer:** **Optically variable ink** that shifts from **gold to green** depending on the angle and lighting. This is especially visible at the level of the **fleur-de-lys**.

---

### Question 12
**You receive a scanned copy of a BC driver's license. How can you check if it's authentic from the scan alone?**

**Answer:** Look for the **laser-engraved signature/photo** which should be visible in the scan. Also check for the **secondary "ghosted" picture** (though this may be harder to verify in a scan). The black and white photo on the front left should be visible. Check that barcode data aligns with printed information.

---

### Question 13
**A Newfoundland license number is G990917999. What format is this, and what can you determine?**

**Answer:** This is **Format 1** (AYYMMDD###):
- **G** = Last name starts with G
- **990917** = YYMMDD → born **September 17, 1999**
- **999** = Unique numbers

---

### Question 14
**What is the "tinny" sound test, and which province is it associated with?**

**Answer:** Some cards produce a **"tinny" sound when dropped** — this is a card quality check. It's specifically mentioned for **older BC cards**. A proper polycarbonate card should have a specific sound and feel. This can help detect cards made from the wrong materials.

---

### Question 15
**Both the Operations Team and the Sales Team validate driver's licenses. True or False?**

**Answer:** **True.** Both teams share responsibility for ID validation. The guide includes sections titled "Details to Be Validated by Operations Team" and "Details to Be Validated by Sales Team."
