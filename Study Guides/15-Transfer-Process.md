# STUDY GUIDE: Transfer Process

### *MHCCA Internal Training Guide*

---

## WHAT IS THIS DOCUMENT ABOUT?

This guide covers the **Transfer Process** at Mitsubishi HC Capital Canada (MHCCA) — what happens when a lease contract needs to be moved from one client to another. In plain English: **Client A has a lease, and Client B wants to take it over.** This is called an "assumption."

Think of it like selling a car that still has payments on it. The bank doesn't just let the new buyer take over — there's paperwork, credit checks, and system updates that need to happen. At MHCCA, three teams coordinate this: **Sales, Sales Support, and Validation.**

**Why should you care?** Transfers are common in commercial leasing — businesses get acquired, partners buy each other out, or equipment gets reassigned. If you mess up the transfer process, you could end up with an unenforceable contract, missing security registrations, or insurance gaps. Know the steps cold.

---

## SECTION 1: WHAT TRIGGERS A TRANSFER?

There are **3 possible scenarios** that trigger the transfer process:

| Scenario | What It Means | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Change of lessee** | Client A transfers the lease to Client B | A trucking company sells its fleet lease to another operator |
| **Equipment changes** | The equipment on the lease is swapped | A construction company replaces a loader with an excavator on the same lease |
| **Payment schedule changes** | The payment terms are modified | Monthly payments restructured to seasonal payments |

> **Why This Matters:** The test may describe a situation and ask whether a transfer process is required. Any of these three scenarios — change of lessee, equipment change, or payment schedule change — triggers the full transfer procedure.

---

## SECTION 2: REQUIRED DOCUMENTS — THE COMPLETE CHECKLIST

Before a transfer can proceed, the following documents **must** be assembled into a complete package. Missing even one can delay or block the transfer.

| # | Document | Details |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Appendix** | According to the file type: **A**, **G**, or **assignment agreement** |
| 2 | **PAD Agreement** | Pre-Authorized Debit form — signed by **Client B** (the assignee) |
| 3 | **E-Sign Summary** | Required if documents were signed electronically via the **One Span** platform |
| 4 | **ID** | For **both** Client A AND Client B |
| 5 | **Insurance documents** | Unless covered by **GAI** (Great American Insurance program) |
| 6 | **Void cheque** | From **Client B** |
| 7 | **Credit decision** | With the **IFL #** (InfoLease number) of the new contract with Client B |
| 8 | **1st page of original contract** | For reference only |
| 9 | **IPC** | Inspection/confirmation document |
| 10 | **REQ/Corp profile** | In Vision's new app — annual filings must be **up to date** for Client B |

> **Why This Matters:** Notice that ID is required for BOTH clients, not just the new one. The void cheque and PAD agreement come from Client B only (they're the ones who will be making payments going forward). And the REQ/Corp profile must have up-to-date annual filings — if Client B's corporate filings are behind, this needs to be resolved before the transfer can proceed.

---

## SECTION 3: STEP-BY-STEP PROCEDURE — SALES TEAM

The Sales team initiates and drives the transfer process. Here is exactly what Sales does:

| Step | Action | Details |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Provide the buyout** | Calculate and provide the buyout amount for the existing contract |
| 2 | **Create documentation and POA** | Use the **automated tool** to generate the transfer documentation and Power of Attorney |
| 3 | **Send request to Sales Support** | Submit the complete package to Sales Support for processing |
| 4 | **Deliver Welcome Package** | Handle delivery of the Welcome Package to Client B, **including the POA** |

> **Why This Matters:** Sales is the client-facing team. They're responsible for the initial documentation AND for making sure Client B receives the Welcome Package with the POA. If Sales forgets to include the POA in the Welcome Package, the client won't have the proper authorization documents.

---

## SECTION 4: STEP-BY-STEP PROCEDURE — SALES SUPPORT

Sales Support handles the system work and acts as the bridge between Sales and Validation.

| Step | Action | Details |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Refer to SharePoint manual** | Detailed instructions are available on SharePoint |
| 2 | **Create the assumption in IFL** | Set up the new contract (assumption) in **InfoLease** |
| 3 | **Match payment grid** | Ensure the payment grid in IFL matches the documentation — **check this twice** |
| 4 | **Provide IFL number** | Give the new contract's IFL number for the package |
| 5 | **Validate documents** | Review all documents for completeness |
| 6 | **Manage communication with Sales** | Coordinate any corrections or missing items |
| 7 | **Send request by email** | Forward the completed request to Validation |

> **Why This Matters:** The payment grid match is mentioned TWICE in the source material — once when creating the assumption and again as a separate validation step. This tells you it's a critical checkpoint. If the payment grid in IFL doesn't match the documentation, the contract terms won't be correct and the client could be billed incorrectly.

---

## SECTION 5: STEP-BY-STEP PROCEDURE — VALIDATION TEAM

The Validation team performs the final review and handles the legal registrations.

| Step | Action | Details |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Receive templated email** | A templated email arrives from Sales Support in the **CoreValidation inbox** |
| 2 | **Access the package** | The package is available in the **V2 folder** |
| 3 | **Validate documents** | Review all documents; follow up with Sales Support if corrections are needed |
| 4 | **Verify payment grid** | Ensure the payment grid in IFL matches the documentation |
| 5 | **Sign and date** | Once validation is complete, sign and date the document |
| 6 | **Amend PPSA** | Make the amendment to the **PPSA** (Personal Property Security Act) registration |
| 7 | **Create Welcome Package** | Include all documents signed by the client (Annex, IPC) |
| 8 | **Send Welcome Package** | Send to the **AM (Account Manager)** |
| 9 | **Loop in insurance team** | CC **assurance@mhccna.com** for visibility on coverage details — **EXCEPT if covered by GAI** |
| 10 | **Save in Vision** | Save the complete package in Vision under the **original file application** |

> **Why This Matters:** Two critical details here: (1) The PPSA amendment is essential — without it, MHCCA's security interest won't properly transfer to the new contract. (2) The insurance team gets looped in via assurance@mhccna.com UNLESS the contract is covered by GAI. If you send insurance notifications for GAI-covered contracts, you're creating unnecessary work.

---

## SECTION 6: VALIDATION IN INFOLEASE — TRANSFER FEE HANDLING

When **Client A is paying the transfer fee**, Validation must verify the charges in InfoLease. Here is the exact system navigation:

| Step | Action in InfoLease |
|---|---|
| 1 | **Validate the charge** | Confirm fees have been integrated |
| 2 | **Contract Section** | Enter the contract number of **Client B** (the new one) |
| 3 | **Select "Charges"** | Validate the type of fee and the amount |
| 4 | **Validate PG and CG** | Check the Personal Guarantee and Corporate Guarantee |
| 5 | **Select Contract Maintenance** | Click upper right corner |
| 6 | **Select Contract Information — Third Parties** | Navigate to third-party section |
| 7 | **Check Related Party Code** | You'll see the name of the guarantor |

**Guarantor lookup in InfoLease:**

| Guarantor Type | Where to Find It |
|---|---|
| **Personal Guarantee (PG)** | CLE contact guarantor |
| **Corporate Guarantee (CG)** | CLE corporate guarantor |

> **Why This Matters:** If there's a PG or CG on the original deal, it MUST follow through to the new contract. Missing a guarantee transfer could leave MHCCA without the security it needs. Always check the "Related Party Code" section to verify guarantor information carried over correctly.

## SECTION 7: KEY SYSTEMS REFERENCE

| System | Role in Transfer Process |
|---|---|
| **Vision** | Documentation, file management, saving complete packages |
| **IFL (InfoLease)** | Contract management, payment grids, charges, guarantor info |
| **PPSA** | Security registration — amendments required for transfers |
| **One Span** | Electronic signature platform (E-Sign summaries come from here) |
| **CoreValidation inbox** | Where Validation receives transfer requests from Sales Support |
| **SharePoint** | Sales Support manual with detailed transfer procedures |

## SECTION 8: THE GAI EXCEPTION

Throughout the transfer process, there is one recurring exception related to the **GAI (Great American Insurance) program**:

- If the contract **IS covered by GAI** → insurance documents are **NOT required** in the transfer package
- If the contract **IS covered by GAI** → the insurance team does **NOT need to be looped in** on the Welcome Package email
- If the contract is **NOT covered by GAI** → standard insurance documents are required and assurance@mhccna.com must be CC'd

> **Why This Matters:** GAI simplifies the transfer process by removing the insurance documentation requirement. But you need to know whether the contract is GAI-covered BEFORE assembling the package. Check the Insurance Tab in Vision — if the GAI checkbox is ticked, you can skip insurance docs and the insurance team notification.

---

## SECTION 9: PROCESS FLOW SUMMARY

Here's the complete flow from start to finish:

```
Sales: Provide buyout → Create docs & POA → Send to Sales Support → Deliver Welcome Package
          ↓
Sales Support: Create assumption in IFL → Match payment grid → Provide IFL # → Validate → Send to Validation
          ↓
Validation: Receive in CoreValidation → Validate in V2 folder → Sign & date → Amend PPSA → Create & send Welcome Package → Save in Vision
```

**Key handoff points:**
1. Sales → Sales Support (via request submission)
2. Sales Support → Validation (via templated email to CoreValidation inbox)
3. Validation → Account Manager (via Welcome Package delivery)

---

## SECTION 10: KEY TERMINOLOGY

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Assumption** | The act of transferring a lease from Client A to Client B |
| **Client A** | The original lessee (the one transferring the lease) |
| **Client B** | The new lessee (the assignee taking over the lease) |
| **POA** | Power of Attorney — authorization document included in Welcome Package |
| **PAD** | Pre-Authorized Debit — the automatic payment agreement signed by Client B |
| **IFL** | InfoLease — the contract management system |
| **PPSA** | Personal Property Security Act — the security registration system |
| **CoreValidation** | The email inbox where Validation receives transfer requests |
| **V2 folder** | Where the complete transfer package is stored for final validation |
| **CLE** | Contact lookup in InfoLease for guarantor information |
| **PG** | Personal Guarantee |
| **CG** | Corporate Guarantee |
| **IPC** | Inspection/confirmation document required in the transfer package |
| **REQ** | Registraire des Entreprises du Québec — Quebec corporate registry |
| **GAI** | Great American Insurance — if the contract is covered, insurance steps are skipped |

## PRACTICE QUESTIONS & ANSWERS

Test yourself. Cover the answers and try to answer from memory.

---

### Question 1
**What are the 3 scenarios that trigger the transfer process?**

**Answer:**
1. Change of lessee
2. Equipment changes
3. Payment schedule changes

---

### Question 2
**Which client signs the PAD Agreement — Client A or Client B? Why?**

**Answer:** **Client B** (the assignee) signs the PAD Agreement because they are the one who will be making payments going forward. The Pre-Authorized Debit must be set up with Client B's banking information.

---

### Question 3
**Name all the required documents for a transfer package.**

**Answer:**
1. Appendix (A, G, or assignment agreement)
2. PAD Agreement (from Client B)
3. E-Sign summary (if signed via One Span)
4. ID (for both Client A and Client B)
5. Insurance documents (unless covered by GAI)
6. Void cheque (from Client B)
7. Credit decision with IFL # of new contract
8. 1st page of original contract
9. IPC
10. REQ/Corp profile (annual filings up to date for Client B)

---

### Question 4
**What tool does Sales use to create the transfer documentation and POA?**

**Answer:** Sales uses the **automated tool** to create the documentation and Power of Attorney.

---

### Question 5
**Where does the Validation team receive transfer requests?**

**Answer:** In the **CoreValidation inbox**, via a templated email from Sales Support.

---

### Question 6
**Where is the transfer package available for the Validation team to review?**

**Answer:** In the **V2 folder**.

---

### Question 7
**What critical step must Validation perform regarding the PPSA?**

**Answer:** Validation must **make the amendment in PPSA** to update the security registration to reflect the new contract/client. Without this, MHCCA's security interest would not be properly transferred.

---

### Question 8
**When should the insurance team (assurance@mhccna.com) NOT be looped in on the Welcome Package?**

**Answer:** When the contract is **covered by GAI** (Great American Insurance program). If GAI covers the contract, the insurance team does not need to be notified.

---

### Question 9
**In InfoLease, how do you find the Personal Guarantee (PG) guarantor for a transferred contract?**

**Answer:** Go to **Contract Maintenance** (upper right) → **Contract Information — Third Parties** → Look at the **Related Party Code** section → The PG is found under **CLE contact guarantor**.

---

### Question 10
**What must be up to date for Client B in the REQ/Corp profile before a transfer can proceed?**

**Answer:** The **annual filings** must be up to date for Client B. This is checked in Vision's new app.

---

### Question 11
**Which element is checked by ALL three teams (Sales Support, Sales Support again during validation, and the Validation team)?**

**Answer:** The **payment grid in IFL matching the documentation**. This is verified at multiple stages because an incorrect payment grid means the client could be billed wrong amounts.

---

### Question 12
**What must Sales include when delivering the Welcome Package to Client B?**

**Answer:** The **POA (Power of Attorney)** must be included in the Welcome Package.

---

### Question 13
**A transfer is being processed for a contract covered by GAI. Which documents can be omitted from the transfer package?**

**Answer:** The **insurance documents** can be omitted because the contract is covered by GAI. Additionally, the insurance team (assurance@mhccna.com) does not need to be looped in on the Welcome Package email.

---

### Question 14
**Where must the complete transfer package be saved after validation is complete?**

**Answer:** In **Vision**, under the **original file application** (not the new contract — the original one).

---

### Question 15
**What is the correct order of the 3 team handoffs in the transfer process?**

**Answer:**
1. **Sales** → **Sales Support** (via request submission)
2. **Sales Support** → **Validation** (via templated email to CoreValidation inbox)
3. **Validation** → **Account Manager** (via Welcome Package delivery)

---

### Question 16
**If Client A is paying the transfer fee, what must Validation check in InfoLease?**

**Answer:** Validation must:
1. Validate the charge (confirm fees integrated)
2. Enter Client B's contract number
3. Select "Charges" and validate fee type and amount
4. Validate the PG and CG
5. Check Related Party Code for guarantor names

---

### Question 17
**What happens if the E-Sign summary is missing from a transfer where documents were signed electronically?**

**Answer:** The E-Sign summary is a required document when contracts are signed via the **One Span** platform. Without it, the transfer package is incomplete and cannot proceed through validation. The E-Sign summary proves that the electronic signatures are authentic and properly executed.

---

### Question 18
**True or False: For a transfer, the credit decision must include the IFL number of the ORIGINAL contract.**

**Answer:** **False.** The credit decision must include the IFL number of the **NEW** contract (with Client B), not the original.
