# STUDY GUIDE: The TAM Program (Travelers Asset Management)
### *Training Guide — TAM OTS (Originate-to-Sell) Program for Sales, November 2025*

---

## WHAT IS THIS DOCUMENT ABOUT?

This guide covers the **TAM Program**, which stands for **Travelers Asset Management**. It is a partnership where MHCCA originates equipment financing deals that would normally be **DECLINED** under MHCCA's internal credit standards, and then **TAM (the funding partner) purchases those deals**.

Why does this matter to you? Because without this program, every deal that gets declined is a deal you lose — and a deal your dealer/vendor partner loses. The TAM Program gives you a **second chance** to get declined deals funded. It turns a "no" into a potential "yes" by routing the deal to a partner with a **broader credit appetite**.

> **The simple version:** Client applies for financing through MHCCA. MHCCA's credit team says "no." Instead of the deal dying, you can submit it to TAM through a specific process. If TAM approves it, the deal gets funded. MHCCA originates it, TAM buys it.

---

## SECTION 1: STRATEGY AND PURPOSE

### Why Does This Program Exist?

The TAM Program was created to solve a real business problem: **MHCCA was losing deals that fell outside its internal credit box.** Dealers and vendors were frustrated when deals got declined, and those deals were going to competitors. The TAM Program addresses this by providing an alternative path for declined transactions.

### Five Strategic Goals

| # | Goal | What It Means |
|---|------|---------------|
| 1 | **Provide financing for declined transactions** | Deals that MHCCA cannot fund internally get a second chance through TAM |
| 2 | **Bring value to dealer/vendor partners** | Dealers hate hearing "declined" — this gives them a solution and keeps the relationship strong |
| 3 | **Drive growth through controlled, risk-adjusted expansion** | MHCCA grows its volume without taking on additional credit risk (TAM buys the risk) |
| 4 | **Differentiate MHCCA as flexible and solutions-oriented** | Being able to say "we have options even if the standard application is declined" sets MHCCA apart from competitors |
| 5 | **Align with MHCCA's MTMP transformation strategy** | This program is part of the broader business transformation initiative |

**Why This Matters:** When you are talking to a dealer or vendor, being able to say "even if the initial application doesn't go through, we have a program that may still get it done" is a powerful differentiator. It makes MHCCA a more attractive partner.

## SECTION 2: THE PROGRAM MODEL

### Originate-to-Sell (OTS)

The TAM Program follows an **Originate-to-Sell** model. Here is what that means in plain language:

- **MHCCA originates** the deal — you (the sales team) bring in the application, process it, document it, and fund it
- **MHCCA retains** the customer relationship and documentation control
- **TAM reviews and approves** transactions that MHCCA cannot fund internally
- **TAM purchases** the approved deals on a **bi-monthly basis** (twice per month)

### Target Market

- **Equipment financing** with flexible terms
- Targeting **near-prime and subprime** credit segments
- TAM has a **broader credit appetite** than MHCCA — meaning they will approve deals that MHCCA's stricter internal credit box would decline

### Key Numbers to Remember

| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| How often TAM purchases deals | **Bi-monthly** (twice per month) |
| Projected annual volume | **$30 million ($30MM)** |
| Maximum days on MHCCA books before sale | **30 days** |

**Why This Matters:** The bi-monthly purchase cycle means timing matters. You need to be aware of the cadence so deals get packaged and sold on time. And the 30-day maximum means deals cannot sit on MHCCA's books indefinitely — they must be transferred to TAM within 30 days.

## SECTION 3: PRICING — THE RULES

### The #1 Rule: Pricing is NON-NEGOTIABLE

TAM's rates follow their **approved rate grid**. You cannot negotiate these rates down. They are what they are. The rates are updated **monthly**, and approved rates are valid for **30 days**.

### Rate Grid by Credit Rating

| Amount Range | CR1 | CR2 | CR3 | CR4 | CR5 |
|-------------|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| **$25K - $39,999** | 10.55% | 13.80% | 17.25% | 20.70% | 22.70% |
| **$40K - $99,999** | 8.55% | 11.40% | 16.55% | 19.70% | 21.40% |
| **$100K - $350K** | 8.35% | 11.20% | 15.95% | 19.40% | 21.10% |

### Key Observations About the Rate Grid

- **Rates decrease as deal size increases** — a $100K deal at CR3 is 15.95% vs. 17.25% for a $25K deal at CR3
- **Rates increase significantly as credit quality decreases** — CR1 is the best credit, CR5 is the riskiest
- These are **higher rates than MHCCA's standard rates** because TAM is funding riskier deals
- **Commission is already included** in the rate grid
- Dealers may add their own commission on top

### Permitted Rate Reductions

There is one exception to the "non-negotiable" rule:

- For **non-HD (non-heavy-duty) transport transactions ONLY**:
  - **CR3, CR4, CR5** deals: **1% rate reduction** permitted
  - **CR2** deals: **0.60% rate reduction** permitted
  - CR1 deals: No reduction mentioned

**Important:** This reduction applies ONLY to non-HD transport. All other transaction types use the grid rates with zero flexibility.

### Documentation Fees

| Payment Method | Fee Amount |
|---------------|------------|
| **Cash** | **$500** |
| **Financed** | **$750** |

Doc fees can be financed, but cash is preferred (there is a discount for paying cash — $250 less).

| Responsibility | Details |

| Responsibility | Details |

**Why This Matters:** The most important handoff for you as a sales associate is the one between you and **Capital Markets**. You do NOT submit to the TAM portal yourself.

## SECTION 5: THE 8-STEP SALES PROCESS

This is the step-by-step process you follow when a deal gets declined and you want to try the TAM route. Memorize these steps.

### Step 1: Submit the Application Through MHCCA's Standard Process

Everything starts normally. You submit the deal through the regular channel. If it gets **approved**, great — proceed as usual. If it gets **declined**, move to Step 2.

### Step 2: Check Eligibility Against TAM Criteria

Use the **decision tree** to determine if the declined deal qualifies for the TAM program. Not every declined deal is eligible — there are restricted industries and other exclusions (see Section 7).

### Step 3: Email Capital Markets Using the TAM-OTS Request Template

If the deal is eligible, send an email to Capital Markets with:
- **Subject line:** TAM Portal Submission Request – [Application Number]
- **Body includes:** Application Number, Credit Decision (attach the decline), Client Name, Amount, Asset Description & Price Check, Other Information

### Step 4: Capital Markets Submits to TAM and Gets Partner Approval

Capital Markets enters the deal into the **TAM Portal**. TAM reviews it and makes their decision.

### Step 5: Forward TAM Approval + Age Term Matrix to MHCCA Credit

Once TAM approves, Capital Markets sends the approval form and the **Age Term Matrix** to MHCCA Credit so they can update the file.

### Step 6: Validate Pricing and Fees

Confirm the pricing matches the TAM rate grid (remember: **non-negotiable**). Confirm the documentation fee ($500 cash or $750 financed).

### Step 7: Inform the Partner (Vendor/Broker)

Contact the dealer or broker. Explain that MHCCA has **broader flexibility** through this program. Be transparent that **pricing is higher** because the deal has a riskier credit profile.

### Step 8: Complete Documentation and Funding

Finalize all paperwork, fund the deal, and confirm the **TAM-OTS designation** is correctly applied. The deal will then be purchased by TAM in the next bi-monthly cycle.

## SECTION 6: GENERAL PROCESS FLOW (THE BIG PICTURE)

Here is the entire flow from start to finish, simplified:

```
1. MHCCA Credit declines the deal
        |
        v
2. Sales evaluates eligibility (decision tree)
        |
        v
3. Capital Markets submits to TAM Portal
        |
        v
4. TAM evaluates and approves (or declines)
        |
        v
5. Sales documents the deal
        |
        v
6. MHCCA funds the deal
        |
        v
7. Bi-monthly sale to TAM (deal transferred)
        |
        v
8. TAM audits and settles
```

**Key point:** If TAM also declines the deal, it stays declined. There is no third option. The deal is dead.

## SECTION 7: RESTRICTED AND BLOCKED LISTS

### Restricted Transactions (NOT Eligible for TAM)

The following are **not eligible** for the TAM Program:

- **Certain industries excluded:** Rental houses, agriculture, underground mining
- **Active bankruptcy situations** — if the client is currently in bankruptcy, the deal cannot go through TAM
- Deals must be transferred to TAM within a **maximum of 30 days** on MHCCA's books
