# STUDY GUIDE: Combo Prospecting
### *By Tony J. Hughes — "The Powerful One-Two Punch That Fills Your Pipeline and Wins Sales"*

---

## WHAT IS THIS DOCUMENT ABOUT?

This is a summary of a sales book that teaches you **how to find new customers (prospecting)** using a combination of different outreach methods instead of relying on just one. Think of it like fishing with a net instead of a single hook — you cast a wide, coordinated net across phone, email, and social media to catch more prospects.

The core idea is simple: **Don't just call. Don't just email. Do BOTH, plus more — at the same time.** That's the "combo."

---

## SECTION 1: THE FIRST RULE OF SALES

### Rule: Prospect Every Day

This is the #1 takeaway from the entire book. No matter what else is happening, **you must prospect every single day.** Not once a week. Not when you feel like it. Every. Single. Day.

**Why?** Because your pipeline (the list of potential deals you're working on) is like a bathtub with the drain open — deals are always falling out (people say no, deals stall, companies go with competitors). If you're not constantly filling it from the top, it empties out fast.

> *"Selling is a blue-collar trade dressed up in Wall Street clothing. You must put in an honest day's work, day in and day out."*

### The Boxing Analogy

Hughes compares sales to boxing:
- **The jab** = your emails, social media posts, text messages (light, repeated touches that set up the prospect)
- **The knockout punch** = the phone call (this is where you break down resistance and secure the appointment)

You need both. A boxer who only jabs never wins. A boxer who only throws haymakers gets dodged. **The combo wins fights.**

## SECTION 2: THE "TRIPLE" — The Core Technique

This is the most important tactical concept in the whole book. A "triple" is three actions done in rapid succession:

### Step 1: Phone Call
Pick up the phone and call the prospect directly.

### Step 2: Voice Mail
If they don't answer (which is most of the time), leave a short, value-focused voicemail.

### Step 3: Email
Immediately after hanging up, send a brief follow-up email.

**Then, if time permits, also send:**
- A LinkedIn InMail
- A text message

### The Numbers You Need to Know

| Detail | Number |
|--------|--------|
| Triples per day | **30** |
| Time per triple | **2 minutes or less** |
| Best time to call | **7:45 a.m. to 8:45 a.m.** |
| Minimum daily calling time | **1 hour** |

### Why 7:45 a.m.?

This is a smart tactical detail. Early morning is when you catch decision-makers **before** two things happen:
1. They get pulled into back-to-back meetings
2. Their assistants/gatekeepers arrive and start screening calls

**Example:** Imagine you're trying to reach the VP of Operations at a trucking company. At 10 a.m., you'll get the receptionist who says "he's in a meeting." At 7:50 a.m., that VP is sitting at his desk with his coffee, and he picks up the phone himself. That's your window.

## SECTION 3: NEVER COLD CALL — ALWAYS WARM CALL

This is a critical mindset shift. Hughes says **none of your calls should ever be "cold."** Every call should be a "warm call."

### What's the Difference?

**Cold call:** You dial a random number and pitch your product to someone who has no idea who you are.

**Warm call:** Before you ever pick up the phone, you've done your homework:
- You've researched them on LinkedIn
- You know their company's challenges
- You've found a mutual connection or something personal to reference
- You might have already engaged with their social media content

### What You Should NEVER Do on Initial Calls

**Do NOT talk about your products or services.** This feels counterintuitive, but it's crucial.

Instead of saying: *"Hi, I'm calling from MHCC, we offer equipment financing..."*

You should focus on: **The business problem you can help solve** — their challenge, their industry, their pain points.

> *"The phone should always be prioritized. If your opportunity is warm, call. If they want to negotiate price, call. If you just got an email in response, call."*

### The 4 Questions to Ask Yourself BEFORE Every Call

These help you prepare your "business-value narrative":

1. **"What's the business problem I can solve for the client?"**
   - *Example:* "They're a construction company growing fast but might be cash-strapped from buying new equipment outright."

2. **"Why should that be a priority for them?"**
   - *Example:* "Because tying up cash in equipment purchases limits their ability to bid on new projects."

3. **"How do I create compelling business case value better than anybody else?"**
   - *Example:* "We offer flexible operating leases that keep equipment off their balance sheet."

4. **"How can I lead with insight to create value in a conversation?"**
   - *Example:* "I could share how similar companies in their space are using financing to scale without burning through cash reserves."

> *"If you create value, they will come."*

## SECTION 4: LINKEDIN SALES NAVIGATOR

Hughes calls LinkedIn Sales Navigator an **"essential sales tool"** — the world's greatest business directory. This isn't optional; he says to familiarize yourself with it even if you have to pay for it yourself.

### What to Use It For

1. **Identify ideal prospects** before you call them
2. **Research prospect backgrounds** — education, work history, interests
3. **Find mutual connections** who could introduce you
4. **Discover conversation themes** — what the prospect posts about, cares about, comments on
5. **Stay current** on prospect's company news and changes

### Why It Matters

An intelligent, strategic study of LinkedIn gives you everything you need to be **relevant** and to **resonate personally** with each prospect. You're not calling blind — you're calling prepared.

> *"Companies that excel at lead nurturing have 9% more salespeople making quota."*

## SECTION 5: PIPELINE MANAGEMENT

### The Core Problem

> An inadequate pipeline is the **biggest problem** the typical salesperson faces.

Think of your pipeline like this: if you need to close $1M in deals this year, you can't just have $1M worth of prospects. Many will say no, stall, or go elsewhere.

### The Magic Number

You need **3 to 5 times your sales target** in qualified pipeline.

**Example:** If your target is $500K in new business volume, you need $1.5M to $2.5M worth of qualified prospects in your pipeline at any given time.

### The COMBO Pipeline Goal

The combo process aims to help you build a pipeline with **up to 3x the qualified prospects you need** to be successful **within two quarters** (about 6 months).

### Your Two Pipeline Objectives

1. Keep your pipeline **constantly filled** with prospects of the highest possible value
2. Do **everything necessary** to maximize your pipeline outcomes

## SECTION 6: MINDSET & PERSISTENCE

### The Persistence Gap

Here's a shocking statistic from the book:

- **Typical salespeople** make only **2 calls** before giving up on a prospect
- Research from TOPO says salespeople need **8 to 12 touches** on average to get anywhere

That's a massive gap. Most people quit after 2 attempts, but success requires 8-12. This means **the salespeople who keep going inherit all the business that the quitters left behind.**

### The Dead Lead Story

Hughes shares a powerful example: A marketing tech salesperson found a dead lead in his company's CRM. This prospect had been receiving automated drip emails for **6 years** and kept replying "Please stop calling!" When the salesperson applied the combo method persistently and creatively, it took him just **3 months to close the deal.** A 6-year dead lead became a lucrative win.

### The Traits of Star Salespeople

- **Tough-minded** — they don't just accept obstacles, they embrace them
- **Work overtime** — they put in extra effort consistently
- **Squelch negative self-talk** — they shut down internal doubt
- **Wade through rejection** — they keep moving forward no matter how many "no's" they hear

### WIGs — Wildly Important Goals

Set goals so big they scare you. Even if you don't hit them, **aiming high guarantees you accomplish something meaningful.** A salesperson who aims to do 40 triples a day and only hits 25 is still doing better than someone who aimed for 10.

## SECTION 7: KEY TACTICAL TIPS (Quick Reference)

### The Vampire Rule of Prospecting
> *"Once they let you in, it's over. Being on-site dramatically increases the probability of revenue."*

**Translation:** Getting face-to-face dramatically changes the dynamic. Once you're physically in their office, the relationship deepens and the sale becomes much more likely. That's why securing the meeting is everything.

### Referrals Are Gold
If you and your prospect know someone in common, **use that connection.** Call or email the mutual contact and ask them to refer you. Go to their office if you have to. A warm introduction from a trusted contact is the most powerful prospecting tool.

### VITO Packages
**VITO = Very Important Top Officer.** Use couriers to deliver packages to senior executives containing:
- Your value proposition
- Case studies of successful customers
- The specific time you will call to follow up

### Sell with Gravitas
Maintain your dignity. Don't grovel. But don't pretend you're on equal footing with the CEO either. Adopt the role of an **equal, trusted adviser** — someone who brings genuine expertise and insight.

### Use CRM with Marketing Automation
For prospects who aren't ready to buy right now, nurture them through automated campaigns so they think of you when they ARE ready.

### THE BIG NO-NO
> **Be passionate, but NEVER come across as desperate.**

There's a fine line between enthusiasm and desperation. Prospects can smell desperation instantly and it destroys trust. Be eager, be excited about the value you bring — but never act like you NEED the deal.

## PRACTICE QUESTIONS & ANSWERS

### Short Answer

**Q1: What is a "triple" in COMBO prospecting and how long should each one take?**
> **A:** A triple is: (1) Place a phone call, (2) Leave a voice mail if they don't answer, (3) Send a follow-up email. Each triple should take **2 minutes or less.**

**Q2: How many triples per day and at what time?**
> **A:** **30 triples per day**, first thing in the morning, preferably **7:45 a.m. to 8:45 a.m.**

**Q3: Why call early in the morning?**
> **A:** Best chance of connecting directly with decision makers before they get tied up in meetings and before their assistants arrive to screen calls.

**Q4: What should you NEVER do on initial prospecting calls?**
> **A:** Never mention your products or solutions. Instead, deliver a "business-value narrative" — connect on a human level and focus on the commercial value you can deliver.

**Q5: What is the Pareto Principle as applied to sales?**
> **A:** 20% of the input invested results in 80% of the outcome.

**Q6: How much qualified pipeline do you need relative to your sales target?**
> **A:** Three to five times your sales target.

**Q7: What is the "Vampire Rule" of prospecting?**
> **A:** "Once they let you in, it's over." Being on-site dramatically increases the probability of revenue.

**Q8: How many touches does it typically take to get results with a prospect?**
> **A:** On average, **8 to 12 touches** (according to the TOPO Sales Development Benchmark Report).

**Q9: How many calls do typical salespeople make before giving up?**
> **A:** Only **2 calls** before they give up.

**Q10: What are WIGS?**
> **A:** "Wildly Important Goals" — massive goals that guarantee meaningful progress even if not fully achieved.

**Q11: What is the "big no-no" in sales?**
> **A:** Being passionate is great, but **never come across as desperate.**

**Q12: What is LinkedIn Sales Navigator described as?**
> **A:** An "essential sales tool" and the world's greatest business directory.

### Scenario-Based

**Q13: A prospect says "I'm not interested." Using COMBO principles, what do you do next?**
> **A:** Don't give up — typical salespeople quit after 2 tries but success takes 8-12 touches. Continue applying the combo method through multiple channels (phone, email, LinkedIn, text). Reframe the approach with a new business-value narrative focusing on their specific challenges. Use referrals or mutual connections to warm up the relationship.

**Q14: Your pipeline is empty and you're starting from scratch. Walk through your first morning using COMBO techniques.**
> **A:** (1) Open LinkedIn Sales Navigator and identify 30 ideal prospects matching your target customer profile. (2) Research each one — note their background, company challenges, mutual connections. (3) Starting at 7:45 a.m., begin your triples: call each prospect, leave a voicemail if no answer, immediately follow up with an email. (4) After each triple, send a LinkedIn InMail and text. (5) Complete all 30 triples by 8:45 a.m. (6) Focus your messaging on business value, NOT products. (7) Track everything in CRM for follow-up.
