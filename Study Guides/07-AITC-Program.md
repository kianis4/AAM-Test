# STUDY GUIDE: AITC Program (Atlantic Investment Tax Credit)

### *MHCCA Internal Training Guide -- December 2025*

---

## WHAT IS THIS DOCUMENT ABOUT?

This guide covers the **Atlantic Investment Tax Credit (AITC)** program at Mitsubishi HC Capital Canada (MHCCA). In plain English: **the Canadian federal government gives a 10% tax credit when businesses buy brand-new equipment in Atlantic Canada.** MHCCA takes that 10% credit and applies it as a cash down payment on Day 1 of the lease -- so the client immediately pays less. MHCCA then claims that money back from the CRA (Canada Revenue Agency) later through its own tax filings.

Think of it this way: imagine you are buying a $100,000 excavator for your logging company in New Brunswick. Normally, you would finance the full $100,000. But with the AITC program, MHCCA says: "We know the government owes a $10,000 tax credit on this purchase. We will apply that $10,000 as your down payment right now, today, so you only finance $90,000." Your monthly payments go down. MHCCA gets reimbursed from CRA later.

**Why should you care?** This is a powerful competitive differentiator. Not every leasing company offers this. When you understand AITC inside and out, you can win deals in Atlantic Canada that competitors cannot match.

## SECTION 1: STRATEGY -- WHY MHCCA DOES THIS

Before we get into the mechanics, understand *why* MHCCA created this program. There are three strategic pillars:

### 1. Drives Growth

MHCCA targets Atlantic Canada regions and qualifying industries to **capture growth opportunities** that other lenders overlook. Atlantic Canada is an underserved market for equipment financing, and the AITC gives MHCCA a concrete reason to be there.

### 2. Differentiates MHCCA in the Market

Most competitors do not structure their leases to pass the tax credit through as a Day 1 cash down payment. By doing this, MHCCA positions itself as a **flexible, solutions-oriented partner** -- competing on innovation and service, not just rates.

### 3. Profitability While Supporting Clients

Applying the tax credit upfront provides **immediate financial relief** to customers, while MHCCA claims the credit later, maintaining profitability. It is a win-win: the client gets lower payments, and MHCCA recovers the money from CRA.

> **Why This Matters:** On the test, if they ask "What is the strategic purpose of the AITC program?", the answer touches all three: growth in Atlantic Canada, differentiation from competitors, and profitability through the CRA claim process. The program also aligns with MHCCA's MTMP (Mid-Term Management Plan) by leveraging partnerships with trusted dealers and partners.

## SECTION 2: WHAT IS THE AITC?

The **Atlantic Investment Tax Credit (AITC)** is a **10% federal tax credit** on qualifying new equipment purchased or leased in eligible Atlantic Canada regions.

Here is the critical distinction you must understand:

| What the AITC Does | What the AITC Does NOT Do |
|---|---|
| Reduces the **financed amount** by 10% | Does NOT reduce the **equipment price** |
| Acts as a **cash down payment** on Day 1 | Does NOT mean the client gets a discount from the dealer |
| Lowers **monthly lease payments** | Does NOT change the total cost of the equipment itself |
| Is claimed by **MHCCA** from CRA | Is NOT claimed by the client directly |

**Example:** A fishing company in Nova Scotia buys a $200,000 vessel. The vessel still costs $200,000. But the AITC gives a $20,000 credit, which MHCCA applies as a down payment. The client now finances $180,000 instead of $200,000. Their monthly payments are based on $180,000, not $200,000.

> **Why This Matters:** A common misconception (and likely test question) is that the AITC lowers the price of the equipment. It does not. The equipment costs the same. The credit reduces the amount being *financed*.

## SECTION 3: WHO AND WHERE QUALIFIES?

There are three eligibility requirements, and ALL THREE must be met simultaneously. Miss one, and the deal does not qualify.

### Requirement 1: Eligible Region

The equipment must be **located and operated** in one of these regions:

| Province / Region | Eligible? |
|---|---|
| **New Brunswick** | YES |
| **Nova Scotia** | YES |
| **Prince Edward Island** | YES |
| **Newfoundland and Labrador** | YES |
| **Gaspe Peninsula (Quebec)** | YES |
| Ontario | NO |
| British Columbia | NO |
| Alberta | NO |
| Rest of Quebec (outside Gaspe) | NO |

**Memory trick:** Think "**NNPNG**" -- **N**ew Brunswick, **N**ova Scotia, **P**EI, **N**ewfoundland, **G**aspe. Four Atlantic provinces plus one specific Quebec region.

> **Why This Matters:** Notice that the Gaspe Peninsula in Quebec is the odd one out -- it is the only part of Quebec that qualifies. This is a likely test question.

### Requirement 2: Qualifying Industry

The client's business must operate in one of exactly **7 qualifying industries**. You MUST memorize all seven:

| # | Industry | Example You Can Picture |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Farming** | A potato farm in PEI buying a new tractor |
| 2 | **Fishing** | A lobster fishing operation in Nova Scotia buying a new boat |
| 3 | **Logging** | A timber company in New Brunswick buying a new skidder |
| 4 | **Manufacturing & Processing** | A seafood processing plant in Newfoundland buying a new freezer line |
| 5 | **Grain Storage** | A grain elevator operation in New Brunswick buying new silos |
| 6 | **Peat Harvesting** | A peat moss company in New Brunswick buying harvesting equipment |
| 7 | **Energy Generation & Conservation** | A wind farm in Nova Scotia buying new turbines |

**Memory trick:** Think "**F-F-L-M-G-P-E**" -- Farm, Fish, Log, Manufacture, Grain, Peat, Energy. Or picture an Atlantic Canadian landscape: a **farmer** near a **fisherman** by a **logger**, next to a **manufacturing plant**, beside **grain silos** and a **peat bog**, with **wind turbines** in the background.

> **Why This Matters:** The equipment must be used **more than 50%** in qualifying activities. So if a client has a truck they use 40% for logging and 60% for general transport, it does NOT qualify. It must be over 50%.

### Requirement 3: New Equipment Only

- Must be **brand-new** equipment
- **Used** equipment does NOT qualify
- **Refurbished** equipment does NOT qualify

## SECTION 4: HOW THE MATH WORKS

This is one of the most testable sections. Walk through the numbers carefully.

### Standard Example: $100,000 Equipment

| Step | Detail | Amount |
|---|---|---|
| Equipment cost | Brand-new excavator | $100,000 |
| AITC credit (10%) | 10% of $100,000 | $10,000 |
| Applied as cash down | MHCCA applies $10,000 on Day 1 | -$10,000 |
| **Amount financed** | What the client actually finances | **$90,000** |
| CRA claim | MHCCA claims $10,000 from CRA later | $10,000 |

**Result:** The client's monthly payments are based on $90,000, not $100,000. Lower payments. Same equipment.

### What If Credit Requires MORE Than 10% Down?

Sometimes the Credit team determines a client needs a larger down payment -- say 15%.

| Component | Amount |
|---|---|
| Equipment cost | $100,000 |
| Required cash down | 15% = $15,000 |
| AITC credit covers | 10% = $10,000 |
| **Client pays out of pocket** | **$5,000** (the difference: 15% - 10%) |
| Amount financed | $85,000 |

The credit approval would state: "Approve with 15% cash down." The AITC covers 10 percentage points of that, and the client pays the remaining 5 percentage points.

> **Why This Matters:** The test may ask what happens when a higher cash down is required. The answer: the AITC credit covers the first 10%, and the client pays the remainder out of pocket.

### What If a Contract Has Multiple Assets and Only Some Qualify?

If a contract includes two or more assets and only one qualifies for AITC, **the credit is 10% of the qualifying equipment only**, not the entire contract.

**Example:** A client leases a $100,000 new excavator (qualifies) and a $50,000 used forklift (does NOT qualify -- it is used). The AITC credit is 10% of $100,000 = $10,000, NOT 10% of $150,000.

## SECTION 5: THE 8-STEP AITC PROCESS

Every AITC deal follows these 8 steps in order. Memorize the flow.

### Step 1: KYC & Initial Assessment

**Who:** Sales team

**What happens:** Sales conducts full Know Your Customer (KYC) and checks whether the transaction likely qualifies for AITC. They look at three things: Is the client in an eligible **region**? Is the client in a qualifying **industry**? Is the equipment **brand new**?

---

### Step 2: Eligibility Confirmation

**Who:** Sales submits to Accounting

**What happens:** Sales submits an eligibility confirmation request to Accounting **via Jira** (under the Accounting section, "Other" category). The Jira ticket title should start with "AITC." The request must include:
- The company registry / industry sector
- Lessee's name
- Equipment details
- Primary location where equipment will be used
- The use of the equipment (activities)

Accounting validates that the transaction meets AITC criteria and confirms eligibility.

---

### Step 3: Credit Approval

**Who:** Credit team

**What happens:** Sales includes the AITC qualification and the 10% cash down in the credit request. Two scenarios:
- If Accounting's eligibility confirmation **is attached** --> Credit issues **final approval**
- If Accounting's confirmation **is NOT yet attached** --> Credit issues **conditional approval** (pending confirmation)

The approval includes the 10% cash down representing the tax credit. The transaction is approved under an AITC program in Vision (standard or subsidized).

---

### Step 4: Documentation

**Who:** Sales / Operations

**What happens:**
- Document the transaction **under MHCCA** (not a third party)
- Include the **Atlantic Investment Tax Credit Appendix** in the lease agreement
- Reflect the 10% tax credit as a **step payment** (first lease payment) in the structure
- Select the correct **AITC program in Vision** (standard or subsidized)

---

### Step 5: Validation

**Who:** Operations

**What happens:** Operations performs full file validation with three specific AITC checks:
1. **Eligibility Confirmation** -- Verify Accounting approval for AITC eligibility
2. **Program Selection** -- Confirm transaction in Vision is booked under an AITC program
3. **Documentation** -- Ensure the lease agreement includes the Atlantic Investment Tax Credit Appendix, and that the contract is documented under MHCCA. The Tax Credit amount should be entered as a Step Payment (first lease payment)

Operations also communicates funding instructions to Accounting via the **booking screen comment in Vision**:
- 10% tax credit applied as cash down (should NOT be PAP)
- Applicable taxes on credit amount (should be paid upfront by client)
- Any other amounts to be collected

The booking screen comment format:
```
AITC
PAP: $ of tx, Other amount to pap if any
TAX CREDIT: $
```

---

### Step 6: Booking & Funding

**Who:** Accounting

**What happens:**
- Accounting books the transaction under MHCCA
- Records the credit amount in **General Ledger account 1827** (InfoLease) during funding
- Ensures Net Fund GL field reflects credit amount before taxes (tax portion paid upfront by client)
- Releases funding

**Important funding instructions for Sales:**
- Do **NOT** collect the 10% cash down payment (it is covered by the tax credit)
- **DO** collect sales taxes on the tax credit upfront from the client

---

### Step 7: Manual Flag Creation

**Who:** Sales requests; Customer Service creates

**What happens:** Once Sales receives the funding notice, they request Customer Service to add a **Buyout Tool flag** to identify the contract for buyouts and payment history. The request goes by email to **service@mhccna.com** with the subject line starting with **"AITC Flag Creation Request"** and includes the app number.

---

### Step 8: Claim & Post-Funding Monitoring

**Who:** Accounting / Customer Service

**What happens:**
- MHCCA claims the credit through **CRA tax filings**
- Accounting reconciles AITC contracts with credits received
- Once the tax credit is received, Accounting adds a note in InfoLease under the department *ACCOUNTING DEPARTMENT*: **"AITC -- CREDIT RECEIVED (contract number)"**
- Customer Service handles buyouts and balance calculations going forward

## SECTION 8: BUYOUTS & THE MANUAL ADJUSTMENT

This is a critical area for Customer Service especially.

### What Happens at Buyout Time?

When a customer wants to buy out their lease early (or at end of term), there is a special consideration for AITC deals:

**If MHCCA HAS received the tax credit from CRA:**
- Check InfoLease notes for the comment: **"AITC -- CREDIT RECEIVED (contract number)"**
- You can find it by filtering notes by department: **ACCOUNTING DEPARTMENT - 04**
- Calculate the buyout using the **standard method** (no AITC adjustment needed)

**If MHCCA has NOT received the tax credit from CRA:**
- There will be NO "AITC -- CREDIT RECEIVED" note in InfoLease
- Calculate the buyout using the standard method **PLUS add 10% of the equipment cost** (before taxes)
- The equipment cost is specified in the contract, in the Atlantic Investment Tax Credit Appendix

### The Buyout Manual Adjustment Tool

Customer Service uses the Buyout Tool to make this adjustment. In the tool:
- Enter the tax credit amount in the **Adjustment** field
- Enter **"AITC"** in the **Reason** field
- Click **Recalculate**

**Example from the training materials:** For a $125,000 equipment deal, the adjustment amount would be $12,500 (10% of $125,000), entered with reason "AITC."

### Why Does This Adjustment Exist?

Remember: MHCCA applied the 10% credit as a down payment on Day 1 -- before MHCCA actually received that money from CRA. If the client buys out early and CRA has not yet paid MHCCA, MHCCA needs to recover that amount. The **Atlantic Investment Tax Credit Appendix** in the lease agreement (Point 4 specifically) states that this amount will be charged to the customer if MHCCA does not receive the credit.

> **Why This Matters:** If a customer asks why this extra amount appears on their balance, refer them to **Point 4 of the Atlantic Investment Tax Credit Appendix**. Always double-check buyout calculations with another Customer Service agent before sending to the client.

## SECTION 9: FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS (FROM THE TRAINING GUIDE)

These are the actual FAQs from the MHCCA training material. Study them -- they are highly testable.

**Q: What is the AITC Program?**
A: A federal initiative offering a 10% tax credit on new equipment in eligible regions and industries.

**Q: Who qualifies?**
A: Businesses in Atlantic Canada or the Gaspe Peninsula using new equipment more than 50% in qualifying activities.

**Q: How is the credit applied?**
A: As a 10% cash down on the first lease payment; MHCCA claims the credit from CRA later.

**Q: What happens if the credit is denied by CRA?**
A: The appendix in the lease agreement allows MHCCA to recover the amount from the client. In practice, MHCCA manages the recovery process and absorbs the initial risk.

**Q: Are used or refurbished assets eligible?**
A: No. Only **brand-new** equipment qualifies.

**Q: If a contract includes two or more assets and only one qualifies, how is the tax credit calculated?**
A: The credit is 10% of the cost of the **qualifying equipment only**, not the entire contract.

**Q: What happens if Credit approves the transaction with a higher cash down requirement?**
A: The client must pay upfront the difference between the required cash down and the tax credit amount. Example: 15% required = 10% AITC credit + 5% client cash.

**Q: If a customer requests a buyout before MHCCA has received the tax credit, what happens?**
A: The tax credit amount (10% of equipment cost) is added to their balance. If the customer asks why, refer them to Point 4 of the Atlantic Investment Tax Credit Appendix.

## SECTION 11: CRITICAL DETAILS THAT ARE EASY TO MISS

These are the small but important details that separate a passing score from a failing one:

1. **Minimum lease term is 24 months.** Not 12. Not 36. Twenty-four months.

2. **Equipment must be used MORE THAN 50% in qualifying activities.** Exactly 50% does NOT qualify.

3. **The Gaspe Peninsula in Quebec qualifies.** The rest of Quebec does not.

4. **The 10% cash down is NOT collected via PAP (Pre-Authorized Payment).** It is covered by the tax credit.

5. **Sales taxes on the tax credit amount ARE collected upfront from the client.** The credit covers the down payment, but not the tax on that down payment.

6. **Jira ticket titles must start with "AITC."** This is the format for eligibility confirmation requests.

7. **Manual flag request emails must start with "AITC Flag Creation Request."** Sent to service@mhccna.com with the app number.

8. **GL account 1827** is where the tax credit is recorded in InfoLease during funding.

9. **The lease agreement MUST include the Atlantic Investment Tax Credit Appendix.** Without it, MHCCA has no legal basis to recover the credit if CRA denies it.

10. **Subsidized AITC programs use the same rate sheets as their non-AITC subsidized counterparts.** Only the standard program (384) has its own pricing grid.

11. **Contract must be documented under MHCCA** -- not under a third party.

12. **The payment history mandatory note** must be added before sharing any payment history with the client. Both Sales and Customer Service share this responsibility.
